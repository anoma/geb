Transcription of the construction from Bunge & Carboni,
"The Symmetric Topos," *Journal of Pure and Applied Algebra*
105 (1995) 233--249, pp. 237--238, attributed to an unpublished
construction of Pitts.

## Overview

Given a category P with finite products, the *equalizer
completion* P\_eq is a category with finite limits together with
a full and faithful functor Delta : P -> P\_eq, which is the free
such over P.

The construction proceeds by:

1. Defining objects of P\_eq as *coreflexive pairs* in P.
2. Defining morphisms via an equivalence relation on
   *premorphisms*.
3. Showing that P\_eq has finite limits, and is the free
   finite-limit category over P (among categories with finite
   products).

## Preliminary: coreflexive pairs suffice

A category with products has equalizers if and only if it has
equalizers of *coreflexive pairs*.  A parallel pair
partial\_0, partial\_1 : X\_0 -> X\_1 is coreflexive when there
exists rho : X\_1 -> X\_0 with
rho . partial\_0 = id and rho . partial\_1 = id.

In mathlib, the walking parallel pair is `WalkingParallelPair`
(`Mathlib.CategoryTheory.Limits.Shapes.Equalizers`), and
coreflexive pairs are characterized by `IsCoreflexivePair`
(`Mathlib.CategoryTheory.Limits.Shapes.Reflexive`).

## Objects

Given a category P with finite products, the objects of P\_eq
are coreflexive pairs X in P:

```text
         partial_0
  X_0  ==========>  X_1  --(rho)--> X_0
         partial_1
```

satisfying rho . partial\_0 = id\_{X\_0} = rho . partial\_1.

### Correlation with our codebase

We define a self-contained structure `CoreflexivePairObj C`
carrying the parallel pair together with its retraction and
the coreflexivity proofs.

## The equivalence relation on hom-sets

Given a coreflexive pair X and an object V of P, define a
relation on P(X\_0, V) as follows.

Given f\_0, f\_1 : X\_0 -> V and h : X\_1 -> V in P, write:

```text
h : f_0 ~ f_1
```

if h . partial\_i = f\_i for i = 0, 1.  That is, h is a morphism
from X\_1 to V whose compositions with the two legs of the
coreflexive pair yield f\_0 and f\_1 respectively.

This relation is reflexive (witnessed by h = f . rho, using
the coreflexivity conditions), but not necessarily symmetric or
transitive.  Let ~ denote the equivalence relation on P(X\_0, V)
generated by this reflexive relation.

Explicitly, f ~ g holds when there exists a finite zigzag:

```text
f = f_0 ~ f_1 ~ ... ~ f_k = g
```

where each f\_i ~ f\_{i+1} means either there exists
h\_i : f\_i ~ f\_{i+1} or there exists h\_i : f\_{i+1} ~ f\_i.

### Correlation with mathlib

In mathlib, equivalence relations generated by a relation are
available via `EqvGen` (`Init.Core`).  The relation itself can
be defined as a structure or predicate.

## Premorphisms

Given two coreflexive pairs X and Y, a *premorphism*
f : X -> Y is a morphism f : X\_0 -> Y\_0 in P such that:

```text
partial_0 . f  ~  partial_1 . f
```

in P(X\_0, Y\_0) with respect to the coreflexive pair Y.

That is, the two compositions of f with the legs of Y's
coreflexive pair become identified under the equivalence
relation generated by Y's structure.

## Morphisms

A *morphism* X -> Y in P\_eq is a ~-equivalence class of
premorphisms.  Write |f| for the class of f : X\_0 -> Y\_0.

### Closure under composition

Premorphisms are closed under composition in P:  if
f : X -> Y and g : Y -> Z are premorphisms, then g . f
(composed in P on the X\_0, Y\_0, Z\_0 components) is a
premorphism X -> Z.

The equivalence relation respects this composition, so
composition in P\_eq is well defined:

```text
[g] . [f] = [g . f]
```

### Identity

The identity morphism on X is [id\_{X\_0}].

## The embedding functor

There is a full and faithful functor:

```text
Delta : P -> P_eq
```

defined on objects by sending V to the trivial coreflexive
pair (V, V, id\_V, id\_V, id\_V) (where both legs and the
retraction are the identity).  On morphisms, Delta sends
f : U -> V to |f|.

Fullness and faithfulness follow because the equivalence
relation on P(V, W) for the trivial coreflexive pair on W
is the identity relation (every morphism is related only to
itself, since h : f\_0 ~ f\_1 with partial\_i = id forces
f\_0 = h = f\_1).

### Correlation with the embedding functor

In our implementation, the embedding is `cpEmbedding`,
sending `c` to the trivial coreflexive pair
`(c, c, id, id, id)`.

## Finite limits

P\_eq as defined above has all finite limits and is the free
such over the category P with finite products.

The finite products in P\_eq are inherited from P (the product
of two coreflexive pairs is the pointwise product of their
components, using the finite products in P).  The equalizers
arise from the coreflexive structure.

## Dualization

The construction dualizes: given a category S with finite
sums (finite coproducts), the *coequalizer completion*
S\_coeq is the free category with finite colimits over S.

If the starting category has all small products (respectively,
sums), then the equalizer (respectively, coequalizer)
completion is the limit (respectively, colimit) completion.
Starting with a small category C:

```text
Fam(C)_coeq  ~=  PC  =  [C^op, Set]
((Fam(C^op))^op)_eq  ~=  [C, Set]^op
```

When C\_r = Fam(C) is lex, the coequalizer completion can be
replaced by the *exact completion*, which does not require
forming a free equivalence relation.

Note: our codebase contains `GebLean/FreeCoequalizerCompletion.lean`,
which defines the category of parallel pair diagrams in C with
commuting-square morphisms.  That construction is *not* the free
coequalizer completion: it requires C to already have coequalizers
(via `CoequalizerData`) in order to produce coequalizers in the
result.  The correct free coequalizer completion would be the
dual of the present equalizer completion -- using coreflexive
pairs in C^op (equivalently, *reflexive* pairs in C, with a
common section rather than a common retraction) and quotienting
premorphisms by the analogous equivalence relation.
