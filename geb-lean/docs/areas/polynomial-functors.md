## Purpose

This area formalizes polynomial endofunctors on slice categories of
`Type`, their algebra and coalgebra categories, the Lawvere theory of
parameterized binary tree objects (BTOs), and the categorical
infrastructure surrounding them: free and cofree fixpoints (W- and
M-types), (co)limit properties, distributive laws, GSOS rules,
presentation of copresheaves via polynomial coequalizers, the free
equalizer completion, and the bar/copresheaf-cover comonad for
projective resolutions.

## Mathematical context

A *polynomial endofunctor* on a locally Cartesian closed category
such as `Type` is one of the form `A вҶҰ ОЈ_{i : I} Hom(B_i, A)` вҖ” a
coproduct of covariant representables. The Grothendieck construction
identifies such functors with objects of the free coproduct completion
of the base; morphisms of polynomial functors are natural
transformations that split as a reindexing of positions composed with
fiber maps. This presentation is due to Gambino and Kock ("Polynomial
functors and polynomial monads", *Mathematical Proceedings of the
Cambridge Philosophical Society*, 2013) and underlies the book
*Polynomial Functors: A Mathematical Theory of Interaction* (Spivak
and Niu, 2024); Abbott, Altenkirch, and Ghani ("Containers:
constructing strictly positive types", *Theoretical Computer Science*
2005) give the container / strictly-positive-type reading, which
coincides in the locally Cartesian closed setting. Polynomial
endofunctors are equivalently presented as W-type diagrams `I вҶҗ E вҶ’ B
вҶ’ I`; the free algebra (W-type, initial algebra) and the cofree
coalgebra (M-type, terminal coalgebra) are standard constructions (see
Abbott et al.; also AdГЎmek and Koubek, "On the greatest fixed point of
a set functor", *Theoretical Computer Science* 1979).

The *Lawvere theory of parameterized binary tree objects* (BTOs) is
the finite-product theory generated by a leaf constant and a binary
branching operation, subject to a parameterized universal property
analogous to that of parameterized natural numbers objects (Lawvere,
"An elementary theory of the category of sets", 1964; Benabou, on
parameterized NNOs; nLab, "natural numbers object" В§with params). The
syntactic morphisms form a category `LawvereBTCat`; quotienting by the
equational theory gives `LawvereBTQuotCat`. The free equalizer
completion of `LawvereBTQuotCat` (following Pitts, as reported in
Bunge and Carboni, "The symmetric topos", *Journal of Pure and Applied
Algebra* 105, 1995) yields a lex category `LawvereBTLexCat`. Partial
equivalence relations (PERs) on the BTO generate a category internal
to the Lawvere theory that provides a model for a realizability topos.

The *free equalizer completion* follows the coreflexive-pair
construction of Pitts (BungeвҖ“Carboni 1995): objects are coreflexive
pairs in the base, morphisms are equivalence classes of premorphisms.
The *free coequalizer completion* is its dual. Both have been
formalized in this area as foundations for the copresheaf-presentation
and the LawvereBT hierarchy.

Every copresheaf `F : D вҶ’ Type` is isomorphic to a coequalizer of
polynomial functors (the density / co-Yoneda formula); the category
`PolyPresentationLoc D` of localized polynomial presentations is
equivalent to `D вҘӨ Type` (SpivakвҖ“Niu, ch. 5). Individual
representables are projective in presheaf and copresheaf categories;
the *copresheaf cover comonad* arises from the projective cover
construction on copresheaves.

The *comonad bar resolution* for a comonad `G` on a category `C`
produces a simplicial object `B_n = G^{n+1}(X)` with face maps
applying the counit and degeneracy maps applying comultiplication;
the simplicial identities follow from the comonad laws.

Polynomial functors are not novel to this development. Mathlib
formalizes them as containers over `Type` (`Data.PFunctor`,
`MvPFunctor`), and the `Poly` library
(<https://github.com/sinhp/Poly>) formalizes the univariate and
multivariate theory within a locally Cartesian closed framework.
What we have not found formalized in Lean or Idris is the generality
realized here: polynomial functors between arbitrary slice categories
(`PolyFunctorBetweenCat`), and вҖ” more general still вҖ” parametric
right adjoints between arbitrary presheaf categories (the
[NNO, arithmetic, and topos area](nno-arithmetic-topos.md)'s
`PresheafPRA`), together with the range of operations transcribed
from SpivakвҖ“Niu. This claim is consolidated, with its search scope,
in [`possible-novelties.md`](possible-novelties.md).

## Modules

### Core polynomial functor infrastructure

- [`GebLean/Polynomial.lean`](../../GebLean/Polynomial.lean) вҖ” the
  foundational module. Defines `PolyFunctorCat` (polynomial functors
  `Over X вҶ’ Type` as coproducts of covariant representables) and
  `PolyFunctorBetweenCat` (polynomial functors `Over X вҶ’ Over Y`),
  together with the equivalence `familySliceEquiv : FamilyCat Type Y
  вүғ Over Y` that underlies the W-type diagram presentation.
  The polynomial-functor presentation via the
  Grothendieck/family-slice equivalence is standard (GambinoвҖ“Kock
  2013; SpivakвҖ“Niu 2024); mathlib has `CategoryTheory.Limits.Over`
  and `Grothendieck` but not this specific polynomial-functor
  category.

- [`GebLean/PolyAdjunctions.lean`](../../GebLean/PolyAdjunctions.lean)
  вҖ” position and monomial functors; adjunctions between polynomial
  functor categories and `Type`. `ccrPosFunctor` extracts positions;
  monomial functors embed `Type` into polynomial functors; free/cofree
  adjunctions and slice-based adjunctions are constructed.
  The free/forgetful and monomial-functor adjunctions are standard
  (SpivakвҖ“Niu 2024); mathlib has `Adjunction` machinery but not
  these specific functors.

- [`GebLean/PolyUMorph.lean`](../../GebLean/PolyUMorph.lean) вҖ” limits
  and colimits in `PolyFunctorBetweenCat X Y`. `polyBetweenProd` and
  `polyBetweenCoprod` construct arbitrary-indexed products and
  coproducts of polynomial functors via position-tupling and
  fiber-coproduct constructions (SpivakвҖ“Niu 2024 В§5.4).
  The (co)product formulas are from SpivakвҖ“Niu 2024 В§5.4; mathlib
  has `CategoryTheory.Limits.Shapes.Products` but not this
  polynomial-functor instantiation.

- [`GebLean/Utilities/PolyCombinators.lean`](../../GebLean/Utilities/PolyCombinators.lean)
  вҖ” combinators for the adjoint triple `ОЈ_f вҠЈ f* вҠЈ О _f` induced by
  a function `f : X вҶ’ Y`. `polyBaseChange`, `polyDepSum`, and
  `polyDepProd` represent base change, dependent sum, and dependent
  product as polynomial functors; every polynomial decomposes as
  `ОЈ_t вҲҳ О _p вҲҳ s*` for a suitable diagram (GambinoвҖ“Kock 2013).
  The `ОЈ вҠЈ f* вҠЈ О ` adjoint triple and the polynomial decomposition
  are from GambinoвҖ“Kock 2013; relevant mathlib material exists in
  `CategoryTheory.Comma.Over.Pullback`.

### Algebra and coalgebra of polynomial endofunctors

The following six modules build the algebra and coalgebra hierarchy
for polynomial endofunctors, from basic definitions through (co)limit
existence and combinators:

- [`GebLean/PolyAlg.lean`](../../GebLean/PolyAlg.lean) вҖ” defines
  `PolyEndo X` (polynomial endofunctors on `Over X` via Grothendieck)
  and `WTypeEndo X` (via W-type diagrams), with `polyEndoEquiv`
  between them. `PolyAlg` (algebras, using mathlib's
  `CategoryTheory.Endofunctor.Algebra`) and `PolyFix` (initial algebra
  carrier / W-type) are the headline declarations. `PolyCofixCarrier`
  and the cofree comonad are also constructed here.
  Polynomial algebra categories via mathlib `Endofunctor.Algebra`
  and W-types as initial algebras are standard (AbbottвҖ“AltenkirchвҖ“
  Ghani 2005; GambinoвҖ“Kock 2013).

- [`GebLean/PolyAlgUMorph.lean`](../../GebLean/PolyAlgUMorph.lean)
  вҖ” algebra and coalgebra combinators: `algPullback`,
  `coalgPushforward`, `algCoprodDesc`, `coalgProdLift`,
  `algEqRestrict`, `coalgCoeqExtend`, and their functor forms. These
  construct algebra/coalgebra instances for composite endofunctors
  from component data via the universal morphisms in `PolyUMorph`.
  Algebra pullback and coalgebra pushforward are standard (mathlib
  `CategoryTheory.Endofunctor.Algebra`).

- [`GebLean/PolyLimits.lean`](../../GebLean/PolyLimits.lean) вҖ”
  `polyToOverFunctor` (evaluating a polynomial functor to a functor
  `D вҶ’ D`) preserves connected limits. The proof decomposes: a
  coproduct of representables preserves limits that commute with
  coproducts (preconnected index categories), using `ccrEvalIndex_const`
  and the `PreservesLimitsOfShape` instances.
  The fact that coproducts of representables preserve connected
  limits follows from Yoneda and coproductвҖ“connected-limit
  commutation; mathlib has `CategoryTheory.Limits.Preserves`.

- [`GebLean/PolyFilteredColimits.lean`](../../GebLean/PolyFilteredColimits.lean)
  вҖ” finitary polynomial endofunctors preserve filtered colimits.
  `PolyBetweenFinitary` restricts to functors with finite fiber types;
  `overFilteredFactor` and the `PreservesFilteredColimits` instance
  are the headline results, proved by the same layered decomposition
  as `PolyLimits.lean`.
  The fact that finitary functors preserve filtered colimits is
  standard (AdГЎmekвҖ“RosickГҪ, *Locally Presentable and Accessible
  Categories* 1994).

- [`GebLean/PolyAlgColimits.lean`](../../GebLean/PolyAlgColimits.lean)
  вҖ” colimits in `PolyAlg P` for finitary `P`. Finitary polynomial
  endofunctors preserve reflexive coequalizers (the argument: finite
  fiber types allow lifting morphisms through coequalizer maps section
  by section); combined with `PolyFilteredColimits.lean` and standard
  machinery this yields `polyAlgHasColimitsOfSize`.
  Reflexive-coequalizer preservation by finitary functors is
  standard (BarrвҖ“Wells; Kelly; mathlib
  `CategoryTheory.Monad.Coequalizer`).

### Polynomial presentations and copresheaf cover

- [`GebLean/PolyPresentation.lean`](../../GebLean/PolyPresentation.lean)
  вҖ” `PolyPresentation D` (a parallel pair `Оұ ОІ : P вҮү Q` of polynomial
  morphisms) and its category `PolyPresentationCat D`. Objects represent
  the copresheaf `coeq(eval(Оұ), eval(ОІ))`; morphisms are commuting
  squares.
  Presentation of copresheaves as coequalizers of polynomials is a
  consequence of the density formula (SpivakвҖ“Niu 2024 В§5).

- [`GebLean/PolyPresentationEquiv.lean`](../../GebLean/PolyPresentationEquiv.lean)
  вҖ” `polyPresentationLocEquiv : PolyPresentationLoc D вүҢ (D вҘӨ Type)`.
  `densityPresentation` constructs the canonical polynomial
  presentation of a copresheaf from its category of elements via the
  density / co-Yoneda formula; the equivalence follows by combining
  this with the colimit decomposition of any colimit as a coequalizer
  of coproducts.
  The density formula (co-Yoneda) is standard and the localization
  equivalence is made explicit in SpivakвҖ“Niu 2024 В§5; mathlib has
  `CategoryTheory.Elements` and `Limits.Presheaf`.

- [`GebLean/PolyCover.lean`](../../GebLean/PolyCover.lean) вҖ”
  representables are projective in presheaf and copresheaf categories;
  `presheafCoverObj` and `copresheafCoverObj` construct the projective
  cover of a (co)presheaf as a coproduct of representables;
  `EnoughProjectives (Cбө’бө– вҘӨ Type (max u w v))` is the headline
  instance.
  Projectivity of representables and the existence of enough
  projectives in presheaf categories are standard (Mac LaneвҖ“Moerdijk,
  *Sheaves in Geometry and Logic* 1992); mathlib has
  `CategoryTheory.Preadditive.Projective` but not `EnoughProjectives`
  for presheaf categories (unverified).

- [`GebLean/CopresheafCoverComonad.lean`](../../GebLean/CopresheafCoverComonad.lean)
  вҖ” `copresheafCoverFunctor` extends the cover construction to an
  endofunctor on `C вҘӨ Type`; `copresheafCoverCounit` and
  `copresheafCoverComult` assemble it into a comonad.
  The comonad structure on the projective cover is standard in
  homological algebra (see also
  `../research/coalgebra-copresheaf-math.md`).

- [`GebLean/BarResolution.lean`](../../GebLean/BarResolution.lean) вҖ”
  `barResolution : C вҶ’ SimplicialObject C` for a comonad `G` on `C`.
  Auxiliary lemmas `skipValue`, `mergeFlat`, `barFace`, `barDegen`
  decompose the simplicial structure; `barSimplexMap` and `barQuiverMap`
  handle the functoriality; the simplicial identities follow from
  comonad laws.
  The bar resolution of a comonad is classical (EilenbergвҖ“Moore;
  Mac Lane, *Homology* 1963); mathlib has
  `AlgebraicTopology.SimplicialObject` but not this comonad
  instance.

### Distributive laws and GSOS

- [`GebLean/PolyDistributiveLaw.lean`](../../GebLean/PolyDistributiveLaw.lean)
  вҖ” the canonical distributive law `О» : T вҲҳ D вҶ’ D вҲҳ T` where `T` is
  the free monad and `D` the cofree comonad of a polynomial endofunctor
  `P`. Constructed via the anamorphism / terminal coalgebra universal
  property (`polyCofixUnfold`); the distributive law axioms are
  verified.
  The canonical distributive law between the free monad and cofree
  comonad of a container is described in TuriвҖ“Plotkin ("Towards a
  mathematical operational semantics", 1997) and related literature
  (unverified).

- [`GebLean/PolyGSOS.lean`](../../GebLean/PolyGSOS.lean) вҖ” GSOS rules
  as bialgebras for the distributive law of
  `PolyDistributiveLaw.lean`. Assembles `GSOSRule`-structured
  bialgebras from `PolyUMorph.lean` combinators, with supporting
  `@[simp]` lemmas for evaluation and reindexing.
  GSOS rules as distributive-law bialgebras are standard (TuriвҖ“
  Plotkin 1997; Bartels 2004) (unverified).

- [`GebLean/Utilities/GSOSRule.lean`](../../GebLean/Utilities/GSOSRule.lean)
  вҖ” abstract GSOS rule in the sense of TuriвҖ“Plotkin, parameterized by
  a signature endofunctor `Sigma`, a behavior endofunctor `B`, a monad
  `T`, and chosen binary products. `idBehaviorFunctor` builds
  `X вҶҰ X Г— B(X)` from chosen products; `GSOSRule` is the structure
  carrying a natural transformation `Sigma(X Г— B(X)) вҶ’ B(T(X))`.
  The abstract GSOS rule is known mathematics (TuriвҖ“Plotkin LICS 1997).
  Used by the tree-calculus area to give its operational semantics.

- [`GebLean/Utilities/LambdaBialgebra.lean`](../../GebLean/Utilities/LambdaBialgebra.lean)
  вҖ” lambda-bialgebra for a distributive law `T вҲҳ D вҹ№ D вҲҳ T`: an
  object with a compatible `T`-algebra and `D`-coalgebra structure
  subject to a pentagonal compatibility condition. `LambdaBialgebra`
  is the structure; `LambdaBialgebra.Hom` packages simultaneous
  algebra and coalgebra homomorphisms; `toMonadAlgebra` and
  `toComonadCoalgebra` extract the components.
  Lambda-bialgebras for a distributive law are known mathematics
  (TuriвҖ“Plotkin LICS 1997; PowerвҖ“Watanabe distributive laws for
  bialgebras).

### Lawvere theory of binary trees

The following six modules define the Lawvere theory of parameterized
binary tree objects, its quotient, interpretation, and primitive
recursion bound:

- [`GebLean/LawvereBT.lean`](../../GebLean/LawvereBT.lean) вҖ” defines
  `HasNNO` (parameterized NNO, transcribed from nLab) and `HasPBTO`
  (parameterized BTO), the syntactic Lawvere theory `LawvereBTCat`
  with objects `в„•` and morphisms `BTMorN n m = Fin m вҶ’ BTMor1 n`
  generated by projections, `leaf`, `branch`, and `fold`.
  The parameterized BTO is analogous to the parameterized NNO (nLab,
  "natural numbers object В§with params"; Lawvere 1964).

- [`GebLean/LawvereBTEq.lean`](../../GebLean/LawvereBTEq.lean) вҖ”
  the equality proof polynomial for `BTMor1`: seven-component
  coproduct encoding reflexivity, symmetry, transitivity, congruence
  for branch and fold, and the two computation rules
  (`btMorEqReflPoly` through `btMorEqFoldBranchPoly`), together with
  `btMorRel` (the generated congruence relation).

- [`GebLean/LawvereBTQuot.lean`](../../GebLean/LawvereBTQuot.lean) вҖ”
  `BTMorNQuo` (equivalence classes of `BTMorN` under pointwise
  `btMorRel`) and the category `LawvereBTQuotCat` with composition
  lifted via `Quotient.liftвӮӮ`; the category laws follow from the
  syntactic laws via `Quotient.ind`.
  The quotient is a standard construction applied to the syntactic
  Lawvere-BT theory.

- [`GebLean/LawvereBTInterp.lean`](../../GebLean/LawvereBTInterp.lean)
  вҖ” the interpretation functor `interpFunctor : LawvereBTQuotCat вҘӨ
  Type u`; `BT.fold_leaf`, `BT.fold_node`, `BT.leaf_ne_node`, and
  `BTMorNQuo.interpU` with faithfulness of the functor (`lawvereBTQuotCat_isSeparator`).
  Model-theoretic interpretation of a Lawvere theory into `Type`
  is standard; the faithfulness result (`lawvereBTQuotCat_isSeparator`)
  is specific to this theory and we have found no such result in the
  literature.

- [`GebLean/LawvereBTPrimrec.lean`](../../GebLean/LawvereBTPrimrec.lean)
  вҖ” every morphism in `LawvereBTQuotCat` computes a primitive
  recursive function (under a GГ¶del encoding `BT вҶ” в„•`) and
  `interpFunctor` is not full (the Ackermann function is not in the
  image, using `Computability.Ackermann`).
  The primitive recursion bound for tree catamorphisms is standard
  (see `../research/lawvere-elementary-recursive.md`); the non-
  fullness argument via `Computability.Ackermann` is specific to
  this development and we have found no such construction in the
  literature.

- [`GebLean/LawvereBTEqCompletion.lean`](../../GebLean/LawvereBTEqCompletion.lean)
  вҖ” `LawvereBTLexCat` (the free equalizer completion of
  `LawvereBTQuotCat`, has all finite limits via
  `EqualizerCompletionLimits`); `lexInterpFunctor` extends
  `interpFunctor` to `LawvereBTLexCat` by sending coreflexive pairs
  to equalizers in `Type`.
  Applying the free equalizer completion to the Lawvere-BT theory
  and extending `interpFunctor` to `lexInterpFunctor` are novel to
  this development; we have found no such construction in the
  literature.

### PER category on binary trees

The following four modules define a category of partial equivalence
relations (PERs) on the BTO and its limit/colimit structure:

- [`GebLean/TreePER.lean`](../../GebLean/TreePER.lean) вҖ” `PERObj`
  (PER on `T`: a `Boolean`-valued morphism `T Г— T вҶ’ T` satisfying
  symmetry and transitivity), `PERHom` (relation-preserving maps,
  modulo pointwise equivalence on the source domain), and the
  `Category (PERCat C)` instance for any `HasPBTO C`.
  PER categories internal to a Lawvere theory of trees have their
  nearest antecedent in the Effective Topos / PER model of type
  theory (Hyland 1982).

- [`GebLean/TreePERLimits.lean`](../../GebLean/TreePERLimits.lean) вҖ”
  finite limits in `PERCat`: `terminalPERRel` (constantly-leaf
  relation); product PERs encode pairs via `branch`; equalizer PERs
  restrict to the domain where two morphisms agree.
  Limit constructions in a PER category follow the standard pattern
  (Scott; Hyland).

- [`GebLean/TreePERColimits.lean`](../../GebLean/TreePERColimits.lean)
  вҖ” finite colimits in `PERCat`: `initialPERRel` (constantly-false
  relation, empty domain); coproduct PERs dispatch on a tag stored in
  `branch`.
  Coproduct and initial PER constructions follow the same antecedents
  as `TreePERLimits.lean`.

- [`GebLean/TreePERLawvereBT.lean`](../../GebLean/TreePERLawvereBT.lean)
  вҖ” converse transitivity in `LawvereBTQuotCat`: quantified Prop-
  valued transitivity implies equational transitivity. The proof uses
  `lawvereBTQuotCat_isSeparator` (from `LawvereBTInterp`) to reduce
  to global elements, then case-splits via `lawvereBT_bool_dichotomy`.
  The transitivity equivalence is specific to the
  `LawvereBTQuotCat` model; we have found no such result in the
  literature.

### Free topos with binary tree object

- [`GebLean/FreeToposBT.lean`](../../GebLean/FreeToposBT.lean) вҖ”
  `FTObjBase` and `FTObj` (two-level inductive-inductive syntax for the
  free topos with a binary tree object, working around Lean 4 mutual
  inductive restrictions): objects including initial, terminal, products,
  coproducts, exponentials, subobject classifier, natural numbers, and
  `bt`; morphisms generated by structural combinators; `BinTree` and
  `BinTree.fold` for the concrete tree type.
  The free elementary topos with a BTO is a standard categorical
  concept (LambekвҖ“Scott, *Introduction to Higher Order Categorical
  Logic* 1986); this two-level syntactic formalization is specific
  to this development and we have found no such construction in the
  literature.

### Type as a PBTO model

- [`GebLean/TypePBTO.lean`](../../GebLean/TypePBTO.lean) вҖ” `Type u` as
  a model: `HasChosenFiniteProducts (Type u)`, `HasPBTO (Type u)` (via
  `BT` and `typeElim`), `HasBoolDichotomy`, and `HasTreeEq` instances;
  witnesses that the abstract typeclass interfaces are inhabited in a
  model distinct from `LawvereBTQuotCat`.
