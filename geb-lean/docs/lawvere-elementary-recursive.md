# Lawvere Theory of Elementary Recursive Functions

## Overview

This document describes a planned development that mirrors the
`LawvereBT*` family of modules (which presents the free Lawvere
theory of parameterized binary-tree objects and interprets it as
primitive-recursive tree functions), but replaces primitive
recursion with *elementary recursion*.  The resulting category
is intended to serve as a generic syntax for programming
languages: a finite-product category, freely extended to finite
limits, whose morphisms are elementary recursive functions
modulo extensional equality.

## Motivation

A generic syntax for programming languages should assume only
as much computational strength as is needed to state typing
judgments and typecheck terms; any further strength risks
making the category inconsistent as a logic under the
Curry-Howard-Lambek correspondence.

The existing Lawvere theory of parameterized binary-tree
objects (`LawvereBTQuotCat`, together with its equalizer
completion `LawvereBTLexCat`) already interprets into
primitive-recursive tree functions.  Primitive recursion
is expected to be isomorphic, as a Lawvere theory, to
parameterized natural-numbers recursion via the invertible
primitive-recursive pairing functions that have been developed
in `NatArith`, `NatElegantPair`, and related modules.
Tree calculus shows that primitive-recursive reflection over
trees is already more computational strength than is needed
for typechecking.

Elementary recursive functions (see the Grzegorczyk hierarchy
level 3, and the Wikipedia article "Elementary recursive
function") form a strictly smaller class that nonetheless
permits:

* encoding and decoding of Cantor/Szudzik pairings (and thus
  encoding binary trees via a Goedel encoding);
* iterating over paired natural numbers (and thus over trees)
  at a bounded tower height, which is what a typechecker needs;
* multiplication, cut-off subtraction, integer division, and
  bounded sums and products.

Friedman's grand conjecture (see the "Elementary function
arithmetic" article on Wikipedia) is additional motivation for
treating elementary recursion as the natural minimum base for
generic syntactic development.

## Relation to the LawvereBT Development

The current development has the following stratification (see
`GebLean/LawvereBT.lean`, `LawvereBTInterp.lean`,
`LawvereBTEq.lean`, `LawvereBTQuot.lean`,
`LawvereBTPrimrec.lean`, and `LawvereBTEqCompletion.lean`):

1. An inductive term type `BTMor1 n` for n-ary tree morphisms
   generated by projections, leaf, branch, and catamorphism.
2. An equality relation `btMorRel` (a polynomial-presented
   equational theory), with soundness and completeness with
   respect to the standard interpretation in `Type u`.
3. `LawvereBTQuotCat`, the quotient category whose morphisms
   are `btMorRel`-equivalence classes of `BTMorN` tuples.
4. An interpretation functor into `Type u`, proved faithful.
5. A primitive-recursion correspondence via the Goedel encoding
   `BT <-> Nat`.
6. `LawvereBTLexCat`, the free equalizer completion of
   `LawvereBTQuotCat`, carrying all finite limits.

The elementary-recursive development should have a parallel
stratification.

## Construction Plan

The development has the following phases.

### Phase 1: Inductive term for elementary recursive functions

Define an inductive type whose closed terms enumerate the
elementary recursive functions on natural numbers, transcribing
the standard definition from the Wikipedia article "Elementary
recursive function".  The basis is

* `0` (zero);
* `succ` (successor);
* `proj` (projections);
* `sub` (cut-off subtraction);
* `comp` (composition);
* `bsum` (bounded summation
  `Σ_{i < n} f(i, \vec{x})`);
* `bprod` (bounded product
  `Π_{i < n} f(i, \vec{x})`).

The motivation for transcribing the Wikipedia formulation
literally, rather than adopting a more reuse-friendly
primitive-recursion presentation with a size-majorisation
witness, is twofold.  First, the literature gives a variety of
inequivalent-looking bases for the elementary recursive
functions; a literal transcription removes any doubt about what
class is being characterised.  Second, the operations `bsum`
and `bprod` correspond directly to polynomial-functor
operations, so a literal presentation will make equivalences
between polynomial-functor algebras and elementary recursive
functions as transparent as possible.

### Phase 2: Extensional-equality quotient

Define the quotient category whose morphisms from `n` to `m`
are equivalence classes of `m`-tuples of n-ary elementary
recursive functions under extensional equality of their
standard interpretations.  Two presentations of the quotient
are possible, mirroring the choice made for `LawvereBT`:

* a direct setoid with relation
  `r f g := ∀ ctx, interp f ctx = interp g ctx`, or
* a congruence relation presented by an equational theory,
  proved sound and complete with respect to extensional
  equality.

### Phase 3: Lawvere theory and interpretation functor

Assemble the quotient as a category with chosen finite
products (the Lawvere theory of n-ary ER functions) together
with a faithful interpretation functor into `Type u`.

### Phase 4: Finite-limit structure as definable subobjects

Rather than freely adjoining equalizers via
`GebLean/EqualizerCompletion.lean`, the finite-limit category
is built as the category of *decidable ER-subobjects*:

* **Objects.** Pairs `(n, p)` with `n : ℕ` and
  `p : ERMor1 n` whose standard interpretation lands in
  `{0, 1}`, modulo extensional equality of predicates.
* **Morphisms** `(n, p) -> (m, q)`.  Equivalence classes of
  `ERMorN n m`-tuples `f` respecting membership, that is,
  `∀ ctx, p.interp ctx = 1 -> q.interp (f.interp ctx) = 1`.
  The setoid on morphisms identifies two tuples when they
  agree pointwise on every context satisfying `p`, allowing
  them to differ off the subobject without becoming distinct
  morphisms.
* **Finite products.** `(n, p) × (m, q) = (n + m, p ⊓ q)`
  with `⊓` the Boolean conjunction (ER-definable as product
  of `{0, 1}`-valued functions).  Terminal object `(0, 1)`.
* **Equalizers.** For parallel `f, g : (n, p) -> (m, q)`,
  the equalizer is
  `(n, p ⊓ λ ctx. ⊓ᵢ eq(fᵢ(ctx), gᵢ(ctx)))`
  where `eq(x, y) = (1 ⊖ (x ⊖ y)) · (1 ⊖ (y ⊖ x))` is the
  ER-definable Boolean equality on `Nat`, and the outer
  `⊓ᵢ` is conjunction over the `m` output components
  (expressible via derived `bprod`).  Membership in the
  equalizer is decidable because the combined predicate is
  itself ER.
* **Finite limits.** Obtained from finite products and
  equalizers via the standard construction.

The correctness argument is direct rather than via a free-
completion universal property.  Finite products are
preserved from the underlying Lawvere theory because a
product of the trivial predicate `1` with itself is still
`1`; the finite-product structure on predicates refines
finite products on arities.  Equalizers exist because
`Nat` and its finite products have decidable equality
definable by an ER predicate, so subobjects cut out by
`eq` are objects of the category by construction.
Together these yield a category with all finite limits.

The full and faithful embedding
`Δ : LawvereERCat -> LawvereERLexCat` sends `n` to
`(n, 1)`.  It preserves finite products and terminal, and
is the identity on morphisms (since the morphism setoid on
the trivial predicate reduces to ordinary extensional
equality of ER functions).

### Boolean convention

Predicates use "1 = true, 0 = false", so that Boolean
conjunction is multiplication of `{0, 1}`-valued functions
(derived from `bsum`) and Boolean equality on `Nat` is
`eq(x, y) = (1 ⊖ (x ⊖ y)) · (1 ⊖ (y ⊖ x))`.

### Phase 5: Internal categorical development

Subsequent categorical structures in this project are defined
*internal to* `LawvereERLexCat`.  Phase 5 proceeds in two
stages.

#### Stage 1: `BTMor1`-analogue as a decidable subobject

Define an internal `BTMor1`-analogue `X` as a decidable
subobject of some `(ℕ, p)` in `LawvereERLexCat`, together
with ER morphisms for `proj`, `leaf`, `branch`, `fold`, and
an internal typechecker `X -> (ℕ, 1)`.  Every constructor and
destructor is an ER term landing in or out of `X`; the
subobject predicate `p` enforces well-formedness.

In this stage, "internal to `LawvereERLexCat`" means only
"an object of `LawvereERLexCat` together with morphisms of
`LawvereERLexCat`" — that is, the minimal reading that the
finite-limit structure of Phase 4 directly supports.
Reflective operations on `X` are morphisms of the category,
so the typechecker applied to the internal representation of
a term is already inside the category.

#### Stage 2: Internal-category structure on the subobject

Upgrade `X` to an internal category in `LawvereERLexCat`:

* `C₀`: an object of arities (either `(ℕ, 1)` directly, or a
  decidable subobject cutting out arity-consistency data);
* `C₁`: the subobject `X` constructed in Stage 1, reused
  verbatim;
* `src, tgt : X -> C₀`: ER morphisms reading off the input
  and output arities of a term in `X`;
* `id : C₀ -> X`: ER morphism building the identity tuple of
  projections at a given arity;
* `comp : X ×_{C₀} X -> X`: ER morphism performing
  substitution of term tuples, with domain the
  source/target pullback that Phase 4 makes available as a
  finite limit in `LawvereERLexCat`.

The unit and associativity axioms are equations of ER
morphisms between subobjects of finite powers of `ℕ`.

The Stage-1 subobject `X` is reused verbatim as the `C₁`
of the internal category in Stage 2; no reconstruction is
needed.  Stages 1 and 2 are therefore compositional: Stage 1
provides the object structure and the destructor morphisms
needed for reflective operations; Stage 2 supplies the
category-structure morphisms without touching Stage 1.

#### Transport via elementary-function-preserving functors

Because every lex functor `I : LawvereERLexCat -> D` into a
finite-limit category `D` preserves the pullback
`X ×_{C₀} X`, the image of the internal category from
Stage 2 under any such `I` is automatically an internal
category in `D`.  Downstream finite-limit categories equipped with a
lex (elementary-function-preserving) functor from the core
therefore inherit their internal `BTMor1`-analogue and its
internal-category structure for free, as the image of the
one defined in `LawvereERLexCat`.

This is the long-term role of `LawvereERLexCat` in the
project: the source of internal structures that transport
into any ER-capable target.

## Design Decisions

1. **Presentation of elementary recursive functions.**  The
   inductive term type transcribes the Wikipedia definition
   literally: generators `0`, `succ`, `proj`, `sub`, `comp`,
   `bsum`, `bprod`, as described in Phase 1 above.  Reference:
   <https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Elementary_recursive_function#Definition>.

   Derived functions already expected to be present as
   definable terms (not as generators):

   * Boolean conditionals on `i = 0` via
     `sub(succ(zero), i)` (the indicator `1 ⊖ i`).
   * Multiplication `mul(x, y) = bsum(proj_y)(x, y)`.
   * Exponentiation with base `y`:
     `exp(y, n) = bprod(proj_y)(n, y)`.
   * Addition `add(x, y) = bsum(g)(succ(x), y)` where
     `g(i, y) = bprod(proj_y)(sub(succ(zero), i))`
     (the conditional `if i = 0 then y else 1`).

2. **Carrier of the standard interpretation.**  The standard
   interpretation of an `n`-ary term is a function
   `(Fin n -> Nat) -> Nat`.  This matches the Wikipedia
   definition literally, so the class defined by the term
   type coincides with the textbook class by construction.
   The tree-level interpretation `(Fin n -> BT) -> BT` is
   left as a derived semantics, obtained by pre- and post-
   composing with the Goedel encodings `encodeBT`/`decodeBT`
   already defined in `GebLean/LawvereBTPrimrec.lean`; both
   encodings are elementary recursive, so the resulting
   functor between the two Lawvere theories is internal to
   the development.

3. **Quotient presentation.**  Direct setoid quotient by
   extensional equality: the setoid on `ERMorN n m` relates
   two tuples when, for every context `ctx : Fin n -> Nat`
   and every projection index `i : Fin m`, the two
   interpretations agree pointwise.  The Lean-level term
   type is kept minimal, carrying just enough syntactic
   structure to present the category; no inductive
   congruence relation or soundness/completeness theorem is
   developed at the Lean level.  The motivation is twofold.
   First, the category-building phases do not need a
   syntactic handle on morphisms — the equalizer completion
   and Lawvere-theory structure are functorial in the
   underlying category.  Second, reflective constructions
   that would require a syntactic handle (inductive term
   types such as a `BTMor1` analogue, typecheckers, free-
   algebra witnesses) are planned to be built *internal to*
   the resulting syntax category rather than as parallel
   Lean-level developments.

4. **Finite-limit structure via definable subobjects.**
   The finite-limit category is built directly from
   decidable ER-subobjects rather than via a free equalizer
   completion.  Finite products arise from conjunction of
   predicates, equalizers arise from the ER-definable
   Boolean equality on `Nat`, and finite limits follow.
   Rationale: the construction is already self-typechecking,
   because every finite limit is concretely witnessed by an
   ER predicate cutting out a decidable subobject rather
   than by a formal equivalence class of coreflexive pairs.
   No separate equivalence proof between routes is needed,
   since only this route is developed.  See Phase 4 above
   for the full definition of objects, morphisms, and limit
   structure.

5. **Phase 5 internalisation: subobject-plus-category in two
   stages.**  Phase 5 proceeds first as Stage 1, defining an
   internal term type (a `BTMor1`-analogue) as a decidable
   subobject of some `(ℕ, p)` with ER constructors,
   destructors, and typechecker, and then as Stage 2, adding
   category-structure morphisms (`src`, `tgt`, `id`,
   `comp`) that upgrade the Stage-1 subobject in place into
   an internal category in `LawvereERLexCat`.  The Stage-1
   subobject is reused verbatim as the `C₁` of the internal
   category; no reconstruction is needed.
   Internal categories in downstream finite-limit
   categories are then obtained as images under
   elementary-function-preserving (lex) functors from
   `LawvereERLexCat`, which preserve the pullback defining
   internal composition and therefore transport the whole
   internal-category structure in one functor application.
