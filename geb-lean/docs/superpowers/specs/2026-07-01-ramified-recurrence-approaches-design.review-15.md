### R14-M1 — resolved

The bullet now assigns distinct ingredients to the two directions.
The `1 + X`-into-algebra leg carries both round-14 devices (the
alpha/beta derived-operation translation and the pairing-based
encode/decode), matching R14-M1's diagnosis that both fed that
direction. The algebra-into-`1 + X` leg names the previously
missing ingredient: the algebra-generic soundness route of section
6.3 instantiated at the given algebra, composed with the section-6.2
definability theorem. This is one of the two repairs R14-M1 offered
("or runs section 6.3's algebra-generic soundness"), and it is
also the route the round-13 text had invoked before the round-14
rewrite dropped it.

Composition check. Section 6.3's route (Leivant III sections 4-5,
algebra-generic in the paper: Lemmas 8-12 and Proposition 13 hold
for an arbitrary free algebra) concludes that the denotation of a
ramified function over the algebra is computable in elementary
time; the section-6.4 landing renders such computation
reference-definable, on term codes. Section 6.2's theorem takes an
ER morphism (the document's reference definition of elementary,
section 6.1) and produces a `RMRec-omega` realizer over `1 + X`.
The composite "6.3 at the algebra, then 6.2" therefore delivers
"the coded conjugate is `1 + X`-definable" provided the coding
bijection of the bullet's transport agrees with (or is
elementarily interconvertible with) the term-code representation
on which the section-6.4 landing operates. That proviso is not
stated; see R15-m1 below for the residual clause. The named
ingredients are otherwise correct and sufficient, and "no new
ingredient beyond the two already planned" is accurate in the
sense the bullet intends (no per-algebra register machines, in
contrast with the Lemma 6 fallback); "section 6.3 applied at that
algebra" flags the genericity the future workstream's design must
preserve.

## New findings

### Minor

#### R15-m1 — coding-map elementary-ness on the numeric side is silently assumed

The converse leg states that "the coded conjugate ... has
elementary denotation by the algebra-generic soundness route".
Section 6.3's route concludes elementary computability of the
ramified function's denotation (on the term-code representation of
the section-6.4 landing); the coded conjugate composes that
denotation with the transport's encode/decode on the numeric side.
Its elementary-ness therefore additionally requires the coding
maps between the bullet's coding bijection and the landing's term
codes to be elementary — a standard fact (the pairing arithmetic
is ER-definable, as the first direction's parenthetical already
records), and vacuous if the coding bijection is fixed to be that
same Szudzik term coding, but the bullet's indefinite "a coding
bijection between the algebra and `N`" leaves it unstated.
Suggested repair, one clause: pin the coding bijection to the term
coding used by the section-6.4 landing, or add "with the coding
maps themselves elementary (the same pairing arithmetic)".
