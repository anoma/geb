## 1. Scope

Sub-phase 6.3 transcribes Leivant III section 5 (journal p. 226):

- **Lemma 12**: a term of `1λ(A)` of height `h` and redex rank `q`
  reduces to a normal term of height `≤ 2_q(h)` in time
  `O((2_{q+1}(h))^2)`.
- **Proposition 13**: the functions over `A` representable in `1λ(A)`
  are computable in elementary time.

Deliverables are placed in a new file
`GebLean/Ramified/Soundness/Normalization.lean`, with the
signature-agnostic term measures (`size`, `height`) added to the
binder-substitution kit under `GebLean/Binding/`, and tests under
`GebLeanTests/`. Topic branch: `feat/ramified-p6.3-lemma12`.

### 1.1 Non-goals

- **Lemma 4 (p. 219 raising/collapse)** is not formalized. Its role in
  the paper's proof of Proposition 13 is the "without loss of
  generality" step reducing an arbitrary target type to `Ωτ → o`; here
  that reduction is expressed by `prop13_elementary`'s hypothesis type
  (section 5.2). Reason: the downstream consumers landed so far — the
  6.4 normalizer and the 6.5 collapse functor on object-sort
  contexts — produce terms of exactly that shape, and the Phase 7
  assembly is planned to consume them in the same form, so the general
  form has no consumer.
- **η-normality.** `Normal` means no β, destructor, or case redex
  (section 4.3). Leivant's redex definition (p. 224) covers exactly
  the application-shaped redexes, so this is the transcription, not a
  weakening.
- **An executable normalizer on `Tm`.** Sub-phase 6.4's normalizer
  operates on numeric term codes; a computable normalization function
  on `Tm` itself would have no consumer (section 6, decision D5).
- **The deferred 6.2 ticket** (`recurBridge` disposition, cast/HEq
  lemma promotions, `sub_op_nullary`, `Represents.lean` file split,
  and the rest): untouched, one concern per branch.

### 1.2 Decisions

The design records five decisions, each with its reason at the cited
section:

| ID | Decision | Section |
| --- | --- | --- |
| D1 | The height bound is reproved here, transcribing Leivant §5 | 3 |
| D2 | Cost model: counted `OneLambdaStep` steps plus size invariant | 5.1 |

D3-D5 arose during design.

## 2. Sources and pinned forms

Primary source: D. Leivant, "Ramified recurrence and computational
complexity III: Higher type recurrence and elementary complexity",
Annals of Pure and Applied Logic 96 (1999) 209-229, DOI
`10.1016/S0168-0072(98)00040-2`. Local PDF at `/home/terence/wingeb/`.
The following forms were read from the PDF for this design (page
numbers are journal pages).

- **Height (p. 211, §2.1).** The height of `a ∈ A`, denoted `|a|`, is
  the height of the syntax tree of `a`:
  `|c_i(a_1 … a_{r_i})| = 1 + max(|a_1|, …, |a_{r_i}|)`, with
  `max() = 0` (so nullary constructors have height 1). Lemma 12's
  proof applies the same tree-height measure to `1λ` terms
  (`height(F^q) ≤ height(D^q) + height(E^q)` for the substitution
  `[E^q/x]D^q`).
- **Order of a type (p. 213, §2.2).** `ord(o) = 0`,
  `ord(τ → σ) = max(1 + ord(τ), ord(σ))`.
- **Redex, normal, rank, redex rank (p. 224, §4.2).** "A redex in a
  term `M` is a subexpression `EF` that can be reduced. `M` is normal
  if it has no redex. The rank of a redex `EF` is the order of the
  type of `E`. The redex rank of `M` is the largest rank of redexes
  in `M`, if there is one, and is 0 if `M` is normal." The definition
  covers application-shaped subexpressions only; an η-contractum is
  not a redex under it.
- **Lemma 12 (p. 226, §5).** "If `F` is a term of `RλMR_o^ω(A)` of
  height `h` and redex rank `q`, then `F` reduces in `RλMR_o^ω(A)` to
  a normal term `F^N` of height `≤ 2_q(h)`, in time
  `O(2_{q+1}(h)^2)`." Proof structure: (i) view `F` as a term of pure
  `1λ` with the constants as free variables; as such it reduces to a
  `1λ`-normal `F^o` of height `≤ 2_q(h)`, citing reference [36] as
  "see e.g."; (ii) the standard procedure eliminates all redexes of
  rank `q` by structural recurrence, producing `F^q` of redex rank
  `≤ q - 1` and height `≤ 2^h`, then iterates for `q-1, …, 1`; if `F`
  is itself a redex `(λx.D)E` of rank `q` then `F^q = [E^q/x]D^q`, and
  this single reduction — copying `E^q` at most `size(D^q)` times and
  copying `D^q` once — takes time `≤ c · size(F^q)`; the total time to
  `F^o` is `O(2_{q+1}(h))`; (iii) `F^o` may still contain destructor
  and case redexes; these decrease term size and create no new
  β-redexes (`case` has type `o^{k+1} → o`), so at most `size(F^o)` of
  them can be performed, in any order, each costing at most the size
  of its input; the final `F^N` still has height `≤ 2_q(h)` and the
  ι-phase costs at most `size(F^o)^2 = O((2_{q+1}(h))^2)` steps.

  Reading note (recorded reconciliation): the statement's letters say
  "a term of `RλMR_o^ω(A)`" and "reduces in `RλMR_o^ω(A)`", but the
  intended calculus is `1λ(A)`: the redex-rank terminology is defined
  for `1λ(A)` (p. 224), the proof opens by treating `F` as a term of
  the simply-typed `1λ` and closes with "a normal term of `1λ(A)`",
  the proof never treats the recurrence-reduction rule that
  `RλMR_o^ω(A)` adds, and Proposition 13 applies the lemma to the
  `1λ(A)` term `F̄ a^{λτ̄}`. The formalization states Lemma 12 for
  `1λ(A)`.
- **Footnote 10 (p. 226).** "The computation model is immaterial,
  since polynomial reducibilities are absorbed here by the constant
  coefficient."
- **Proposition 13 (p. 226, §5).** "The functions over `A`
  representable in `1λ(A)` are computable in elementary time." Proof:
  for unary `f` defined by a term `F`, Lemma 4 licenses assuming
  `F : Ωτ → o`, so `F̄ : A[τ̄] → o`; for each `a ∈ A`,
  `F̄ a^{λτ̄} = fa`, so computing `fa` reduces to normalizing
  `F̄ a^{λτ̄}`, which by Lemma 12 costs time elementary in the size of
  `F̄ a^{λτ̄}`; since `F̄` is constant, elementary in the size of `a`.
- **Reference [36].** R. Statman, "The typed λ-calculus is not
  elementary recursive", Theoretical Computer Science 9 (1979) 73-81.
  This is the lower-bound companion; Leivant cites it as "see e.g."
  for the upper bound, whose content his own proof paragraph (ii)
  supplies inline.

## 3. Research summary (gate decision 1: source of the height bound)

The parent sub-plan gates 6.3 on deciding whether mathlib, CSLib, or a
transcribable published proof supplies the rank-stratified
normalization bound, or whether it is reproved here. Findings:

- **mathlib** contains no object-level λ-calculus: the only matches
  for β-reduction are elaborator-level utilities
  (`Mathlib.Tactic.DefEqTransformations`, `Mathlib.Util.TermReduce`).
  Nothing to instantiate.
- **CSLib** (already a lake dependency, pinned per
  `docs/lean-resources.md`) has locally-nameless untyped λ-calculus
  with full-β confluence and a locally-nameless simply-typed λ-calculus
  with type safety only
  (`Cslib.Languages.LambdaCalculus.LocallyNameless.Stlc.{Basic,Safety}`).
  No strong normalization, no quantitative bounds; and the
  representation (locally nameless, extrinsic typing) differs from
  this repository's intrinsically-typed `BinderSig` terms, so even an
  existing bound would require a representation transfer costing more
  than the direct proof.
- **Published proofs.** The upper bound is standard: detailed
  treatments include H. Schwichtenberg, "An upper bound for reduction
  sequences in the typed λ-calculus", Archive for Mathematical Logic
  30 (1991) 405-408; A. Beckmann, "Exact bounds for lengths of
  reductions in typed λ-calculus", Journal of Symbolic Logic 66 (2001)
  1277-1285; and P. Battyányi, K. Nour, "An estimation for the lengths
  of reduction sequences of the λμρθ-calculus", arXiv `1703.05930`,
  which proves `tower(k+1, (2k+3)·comp(M))` bounds on all reduction
  sequences by rank elimination, for a calculus containing the plain
  λ-calculus as a fragment (located via the `theoremsearch` MCP).

**Decision D1 (recorded reason).** The bound is proved in this
repository, transcribing Leivant's own §5 rank-elimination recurrence,
which is self-contained for exactly the calculus at hand (with
destructor and case constants). The published treatments above are
cited as the detailed literature for the method; none applies
wholesale, since each is stated for a different calculus and syntax
representation, while the proof here must land on
`Tm (oneLambdaSig A)`. No library supplies the result.

## 4. Definitions layer

### 4.1 Generic term measures (`GebLean/Binding/`)

`size` and `height` are signature-agnostic structural measures on the
binder-substitution kit's terms, placed with the kit for reuse:

```lean
def Binding.Tm.size {κ} {S : BinderSig κ} {Γ s} : Tm S Γ s → ℕ
def Binding.Tm.height {κ} {S : BinderSig κ} {Γ s} : Tm S Γ s → ℕ
```

`size` is the node count (variables and operations count 1, an
operation node adds the sizes of its children); `height` is the tree
height in the p. 211 form (`1 + max` over children, `max () = 0`).
Supporting lemmas (exact statements at the plan step): behavior under
`ren` (both measures are renaming-invariant) and under
`instantiate₁` — `height (instantiate₁ N b) ≤ height b + height N`
and the corresponding multiplicative `size` bound, the two facts
Lemma 12's paragraph (ii) consumes. Transcription of the p. 211
height; `size` and the kit packaging are novel packaging.

### 4.2 Order and rank (`Normalization.lean`)

```lean
def RType.ord : RType → ℕ
```

transcribing `ord(o) = 0`, `ord(τ → σ) = max(1 + ord(τ), ord(σ))`
(p. 213). Terms of `1λ(A)` have simple types (`RType.IsSimple`), so no
`Omega` case arises on them; the `Omega` clause of the total function
is fixed at the plan step (candidate: `ord (Ωτ) = ord τ`) and is
irrelevant to the bound.

```lean
def OneLambda.redexRank {A Γ s} (t : Binding.Tm (oneLambdaSig A) Γ s) : ℕ
```

transcribing p. 224: the maximum over redexes of the order of the
applied subterm's type, 0 if none. A redex is a genuine reducible
application — a β-redex `app' (lam' b) N`, a destructor applied to a
constructor word, or a saturated case over a constructor word — not an
arbitrary application node (otherwise normal constructor words would
have positive rank and the `redexRank`-of-normal-is-0 clause of the
source definition would fail).

Implementation subtlety (recorded for the plan step): under `ord`,
every destructor or case redex has rank exactly 1 (`ord (o → o) = 1`),
while Leivant's paragraph (ii) operates in pure `1λ` where the
constants are free variables, so the rank-elimination cycles see only
β-redexes and the ι-redexes are deferred to paragraph (iii). The Lean
`redexRank` transcribes the p. 224 definition (all redexes); the cycle
lemma's internal invariant tracks the β-redex rank only. The two agree
on the hypotheses and conclusions of `lemma12` up to the `q = 0` and
`q = 1` edge cases, which the plan step states explicitly.

### 4.3 Normality

```lean
def OneLambda.Normal {A Γ s} (t : Binding.Tm (oneLambdaSig A) Γ s) : Prop
```

no subterm of `t` is a β, destructor, or case redex. This is the
p. 224 definition ("no redex", with redexes application-shaped); η is
not a redex under it. Two consequences recorded here because
downstream phases rely on them: a closed `Normal` term of type `o` is
a constructor word (its head must be a constant; a `dstr` or `case`
head over a constructor-word scrutinee would itself be a redex), which
is what Proposition 13's read-off and the 6.4 normalizer consume; and
`Normal` terms are exactly the `OneLambdaStep`-irreducible ones up to
the η rule, which plays no role in sections 5-6 of the paper.

### 4.4 Tower arithmetic

Reused from Phase 5: `tower` and the clock-format lemmas of
`GebLean/Ramified/Definability/Bounds.lean` (`add_le_tower`,
`tower_add_le_tower`, `clock_mono`, …). No new tower infrastructure is
expected beyond corollaries in the same style.

## 5. Statements

### 5.1 Lemma 12 (gate decision 2: the cost model)

**Decision D2 (recorded reason).** The time bound is stated as a count
of `OneLambdaStep` steps, using CSLib's counted-chain relation
`Relation.RelatesInSteps` (reuse of an existing abstraction; it
carries bridges to `Relation.ReflTransGen`, the form 6.2's reduction
statements use), together with a size invariant on the intermediate
terms. Leivant charges a β-step in proportion to the result's size;
footnote 10 states the computation model is immaterial at elementary
scale, and the counted-steps-plus-size-invariant form is exactly what
sub-phase 6.4 consumes (the clock of the code-level normalizer is an
iteration count, and the code sizes are bounded by the term sizes).
The size-weighted accounting is therefore not transcribed.

Statement shape (constants and exact tower shapes fixed at the plan
step; `B`, `S`, `H` denote closed clock-format expressions in the
`Bounds.lean` style):

```lean
theorem OneLambda.lemma12 {A Γ s} (t : Binding.Tm (oneLambdaSig A) Γ s) :
    ∃ (n : Binding.Tm (oneLambdaSig A) Γ s) (k : ℕ),
      Normal n ∧
      Relation.RelatesInSteps
        (fun a b => OneLambdaStep a b ∧ Tm.size b ≤ S (redexRank t) (height t))
        t n k ∧
      Tm.height n ≤ H (redexRank t) (height t) ∧
      k ≤ B (redexRank t) (height t)
```

with `H q h` the paper's `2_q(h)` up to fixed constant slack
(`c · tower q (h + c)` format), and `B q h` the paper's
`O((2_{q+1}(h))^2)` likewise. The statement is generic in the algebra
`A`, as `OneLambdaStep` is. The size invariant is expressed by
instantiating the generic counted-chain relation at the intersection
relation (a step whose target also satisfies the size bound), avoiding
a new inductive; the plan step may substitute an equivalent device if
this one elaborates poorly. The intersection relation constrains only
the target of each step; the initial term is covered separately by
`Tm.size t ≤ 2_1(Tm.height t)` (the operations of `oneLambdaSig` have
arity at most two), which any clock-format `S` dominates. The paper's
bare `2_q(h)` absorbs constants
(footnote 10 and the `O(·)`); the acceptance criterion for the bound
shapes is that for each fixed `q` they are elementary in `h`, which is
the only property Proposition 13 and Phase 6.4 consume.

### 5.2 Proposition 13

Statement shape (exact form at the plan step):

```lean
theorem prop13_elementary {τ : RType}
    (F : Binding.Tm (rlmrOSig natAlgSig) []
      (RType.arrow (RType.omega τ) RType.o)) :
    ∃ c : ℕ, ∀ a : FreeAlg natAlgSig,
      -- the application of `barTm F` to `bbRep a` reduces, in at most
      -- `tower c (Tm.height (bbRep a …) + c)` counted steps, to the
      -- constructor word of the denotation of `F` at `a`
      …
```

The statement is at `natAlgSig`, matching its 6.2 anchors (`barTm`,
`bbRep`, `prop11_represents` are declared at `natAlgSig`, not generic
in the algebra); generalizing the 6.2 layer is out of scope. The
hypothesis type `Ωτ → o` expresses the paper's Lemma 4 "without loss
of generality" step (section 1.1). The denotational anchor is
`appEval` through `prop11_represents`, consistent with Decision 2 of
the parent plan (internal-only use of `Represents`/`appEval`; the
Phase 7 final theorem does not mention them). The conclusion's
constant `c` depends only on `F` (its height and redex rank), which is
the paper's "since `F̄` is constant" step.

The parent sub-plan's interface names are kept: `height`, `redexRank`,

## 6. Proof architecture for Lemma 12 (decision D5)

**Decision D5 (recorded reason).** Relational rank-elimination cycles,
following the source's proof paragraph by paragraph; each stage is an
independently committable lemma, matching the commit-first discipline
the parent plan mandates for this sub-phase (the workstream's single
hardest proof). A functional staged normalizer on `Tm` was rejected
for lack of a consumer (6.4 normalizes numeric codes, not `Tm`); a
single well-founded `(rank, size)` measure induction was rejected as
monolithic — it merges the source's separate accounting steps and
resists incremental commits.

Stages, in dependency order:

1. **Substitution bounds (generic, `GebLean/Binding/`).**
   `height (instantiate₁ N b) ≤ height b + height N`; `size` analogue
   (multiplicative); renaming invariance of both measures. These are
   the kit-level facts paragraph (ii) consumes.
2. **Rank-cycle lemma.** By structural induction on the term: if every
   β-redex of `t` has rank `≤ q` (with `q ≥ 1`), then `t` reduces (in
   counted steps, sizes tracked) to a term whose β-redexes all have
   rank `≤ q - 1`, with height at most exponentiated
   (`height ≤ 2^{height t}` up to slack) and steps/sizes in the
   corresponding clock format. The induction mirrors the source's "the
   elimination of redexes of rank `q` proceeds by recurrence on `F`":
   contract the top redex if `t` is itself a rank-`q` β-redex (using
   stage 1 for the height of `[E^q/x]D^q`), otherwise recurse into the
   children and reassemble by the congruence rules
   (`appL`/`appR`/`lamBody` and the existing `reduces_*` lifts).
3. **Downward induction on `q`.** Compose stage-2 cycles for
   `q, q-1, …, 1` into: every term reduces to a β-normal term of
   height `≤ H (redexRank t) (height t)` within the tower-format step
   and size bounds. The tower arithmetic is `Bounds.lean` reuse.
4. **ι-phase.** By strong induction on `size`: a β-normal term reduces
   to a `Normal` term with no new β-redexes created (the destructor
   and case contractions strictly decrease size — each replaces an
   application spine by one of its immediate constituents or a
   branch — and preserve β-normality), in at most `size` steps, with
   the height bound preserved. This is paragraph (iii); the
   no-new-β-redexes observation is the source's parenthetical "recall
   that `case : o^{k+1} → o`". The paper states that these reductions
   "decrease term size"; the strict form, which the size induction
   requires, is a strengthening proved here (it holds per contraction
   by the shape of the `dstrHit`/`dstrMiss`/`case` rules).
5. **Assembly (`lemma12`).** Chain stages 3 and 4; convert between the
   counted-chain and `ReflTransGen` forms where consumers need it.
6. **Proposition 13.** Instantiate `lemma12` at `barTm F` applied to
   `bbRep a`, anchor the normal form through `prop11_represents` and
   the constructor-word consequence of section 4.3, and package the
   bound with the constant-in-`F` separation.

Each stage is one or more commits, each passing the pre-commit triad;
stage 2 is the expected resource limit and is planned for further
internal decomposition at the plan step (per-constructor case lemmas
in the 6.2.10 style if needed).

## 7. Acceptance

- An `example` instantiating `lemma12` on a small closed term with a
  single β-redex (e.g. an identity abstraction applied to a nullary
  constructor word), exhibiting the normal form and checking the
  numeric bound instances. Precedent: the 6.2 zero-constructor smoke
  test, ratified at the 6.2 push review.
- An `example` consuming `prop13_elementary` for a concrete `F`
  (candidate: a Phase 6.1 `prop7Translate` image if a suitable closed
  term is available at execution time; otherwise the smallest closed
  `F : Ωτ → o`, with the choice recorded in the plan).
- `lean_verify` axiom cleanliness (`propext`, `Classical.choice`,
  `Quot.sound` only) on `lemma12` and `prop13_elementary`; the
  pre-commit triad on every commit; `lake build GebLeanAxiomChecks`.

## 8. Process

- This design is adversarially reviewed to convergence before user
  review (project process; review files
  `2026-07-06-ramified-p6.3-lemma12-design.review-N.md`,
  markdownlint-clean).

  clock-format shapes `B`, `S`, `H`; the `Omega` clause of `ord`; the

  implementer per chunk, per-task reviews, whole-branch review before
  the push-review handoff (the 6.2 pattern).
