## Part 1 — source claims against Leivant III

### 1(a) — section 2.4(5), p. 216: pass

The paper's Example 2.4(5) (*Exponentiation in arbitrary* `A`)
reads: since `A` is assumed infinite, it has some 0-ary
constructor `alpha` and some constructor `beta` of arity `> 0`;
if `arity(beta) > 1` then `beta(a)` abbreviates
`beta(a, alpha ... alpha)`; the numeric functions `exp` and `2_m`
"can be simulated over `A` using `alpha` and `beta` in place of
`0` and `s`, respectively." The bullet's description — a unary
sub-algebra generated by a 0-ary and a positive-arity constructor,
simulating `0` and `s` — matches. Section 2.4(6) (p. 217) and
Lemma 6's proof (p. 221, `beta^[n] alpha` as the clock value, `#s`
codes for states) confirm the paper itself deploys the device for
numeric values inside `RMRec-omega(A)`.

### 1(b) — no two-system comparison: pass

Theorem 14 (p. 227) quantifies over one algebra: "Let `A` be a
free algebra. The following conditions are equivalent for a
function `f` over `A`" — five conditions, all over the same `A`
(one machine model and four calculi). No statement in the paper
relates `RMRec-omega(A)` to `RMRec-omega(B)` for distinct algebras
`A`, `B`. Section 6.2 (pp. 227-228) is a prose sketch of a
*multi-sorted* generalization — data systems reduced to sorted
structures with free algebras as sorts, and four modifications to
the type system (`Omega^beta` per sort, etc.) — not an equivalence
or comparison between the single-sorted ramified systems over two
algebras. Lemma 5 (p. 220) compares machine models (Turing
machines versus register machines over algebras), not ramified
systems. The bullet's status claim "never compares two ramified
systems directly" is accurate, and its use of Lemma 5 "only as
motivation for the coded viewpoint" is a faithful description of
what Lemma 5 provides.

### 1(c) — Lemma 5's proof: pass

P. 220, immediately after the statement of Lemma 5: "The proof of
the lemma is straightforward." No proof follows (the text proceeds
to the corollary sentence about poly-time/exponential-time
equivalence and then to section 3.2). Footnote 8 records that the
exponential factor for tree algebras is essential. The bullet's
"the paper declares Lemma 5's proof straightforward and omits it"

### 1(d) — Lemma 6: pass

Pp. 220-221: Lemma 6 is proved in full over a deterministic
register machine `M` over `A` — `alpha` and `beta` "as in Example
2.4(5)", the functions `stt` and `[phi]` defined by simultaneous
recurrence with displayed clauses for `alpha` and `beta` (all

`sz`, `x`, and the coercion `delta`. "Lemma 6, spelled out" and
"Lemma 6 transcribed over register machines for the given algebra"
are accurate descriptions of the statement and of what the
fallback would transcribe.

## Part 2 — internal soundness of the sketch

### 2(a) — the theory-morphism clause: see R14-m1

The constructor assignment underlying direction (i) is
`0 |-> alpha`, `s |-> beta(-, alpha, ..., alpha)`: the image of
`s` is a *derived operation* (a constructor applied with its
remaining arguments filled by the closed term `alpha`), not a
constructor. A morphism of signature functors `1 + X -> F` in the
sense of section 4.3's algebra-map bullet ("a morphism of
signature functors ... induces a functor of syntactic categories")
maps summands to summands and cannot express the `alpha`-filling,
which requires the free algebra (or term monad), not the signature
functor. Additionally, transporting a `1 + X` recurrence instance
requires supplying clauses at `A`'s other constructors (the paper
sets them arbitrarily — Lemma 6's footnote 9 — using the canonical
functionals `C^tau` of section 2.4's preamble); that step is part
of the morphism's action on the recurrence schema, not on the
signature. The notion needed is a theory morphism in the general
Lawvere sense (operations to derived operations, schema instances
to schema instances), which the document nowhere sets up; section
4.3 is its only inter-system-functor inventory. See R14-m1 for the
suggested hedge. The construction itself is sound — the paper's
own Lemma 6 proof and section 2.4(5)-(6) exercise exactly these
ingredients inside `RMRec-omega(A)`.

### 2(b) — "elementary code growth": pass

For iterated Szudzik pairing, `|pair(x, y)|` is at most about
`2 * max(|x|, |y|)` in bit length, so the code of a term with `n`
constructor occurrences has bit length `2^{O(n)}` and magnitude
within a fixed exponential tower of `n` — elementary in the term
size. This matches the paper's own accounting: Lemma 5 records an
exponential-time factor for tree-algebra coding, with footnote 8
noting the exponential is essential (a register machine over
binary trees builds a full tree with `2^n` nodes in linear time),
and the round-13 text already recorded that elementary absorbs it.
The phrase is plausible as stated.

### 2(c) — the repository claim: pass

`GebLean/Utilities/Tupling.lean` exists; its header describes
fixed-length k-tuple Szudzik pairing (`[[z_1,...,z_k]]^{(k)}` via
iterated left-associated Szudzik pairing), with the underlying
sequence machinery in `Utilities/SzudzikSeq.lean`. "The
repository's Szudzik machinery, `GebLean/Utilities/Tupling.lean`,
on the ER side" is accurate. `erKSimEquiv` exists in
`GebLean/LawvereERKSim/Equivalence.lean`, matching the packaging
reference.

## New findings

### Major

#### R14-M1 — both named devices feed one direction of the equivalence

The bullet asserts "The two directions are definability transports
across a coding bijection between the algebra and `N`", assigning
(i) the theory morphism and (ii) the encode/decode pair to the two
directions. As written, both devices construct only the
`1 + X`-to-`A` leg. Direction (i) sends numeric definables into
the `A`-system. Direction (ii)'s encode and decode consume and
produce `A`-elements, so they are definable in the `A`-system (by
structural recursion over `A` and over its unary sub-algebra
respectively); together with (i) they show that the coded
conjugate of every `1 + X`-definable function is `A`-definable.
The converse containment — that the coded conjugate of every
`A`-definable function is `1 + X`-definable, which a categorical
equivalence of the fragments requires and which is the
definability content of the corollary's soundness direction — has
no named ingredient: it needs a numeric interpretation of the
`A`-system's schemata on codes (coded constructors, destructors,
and case from the pairing arithmetic; `A`-recurrence instances
simulated by ramified recurrence on codes, with the growth bound
of 2(b)), or an appeal to the algebra-generic normalization route
of section 6.3, which the round-13 text invoked and this rewrite
dropped. Suggested repair (one or two clauses): name the missing
leg — e.g. re-describe (i) and (ii) as the ingredients of the
functor from the `1 + X` fragment, and add that the converse
direction interprets the `A`-system's schemata on codes via the
same pairing arithmetic (or runs section 6.3's algebra-generic
soundness), whichever the future workstream is intended to design.

### Minor

#### R14-m1 — "theory morphism" exceeds the section 4.3 inventory

"The unary sub-algebra ... carries a theory morphism from the
`1 + X` system" is coherent under the general Lawvere-theory
notion (operations to derived operations), but the document's only
inter-system functoriality (section 4.3) is "a morphism of
signature functors ... induces a functor of syntactic categories",
which does not cover this map: `s |-> beta(-, alpha, ..., alpha)`
fills `beta`'s remaining arguments with the 0-ary constructor
(a derived operation, not a signature-functor component), and the
transport of recurrence instances must set clauses at `A`'s other
constructors (the paper's footnote 9 device). Suggested hedge:
qualify the clause, e.g. "carries a theory morphism from the
`1 + X` system (constructors to derived operations — `beta`'s
remaining arguments filled with `alpha`, recurrence clauses at the
other constructors set arbitrarily, per Lemma 6's proof — a wider
notion than section 4.3's signature-functor morphisms)".

#### R14-m2 — "this workstream's `1 + X` to `LawvereERCat` equivalence"

Within a sentence whose subject is a "categorical equivalence"
between fragments, this phrase invites reading the workstream's
in-scope deliverable as an equivalence of categories. Section 6.1
disclaims exactly that: fullness of `collapseFunctor` is false,
the theorem is the two statements (soundness functor plus
definability existential), and a categorical packaging is open
question 7, not an assertion. The document does use "the
equivalence" loosely elsewhere for the two-statement
characterization (section 5's heading; section 5.2), so the intent
is recoverable, and composing a fragment equivalence with the two
statements does yield the corollary's statements. Suggested
wording: "composing with this workstream's characterization of the
`1 + X` system against `LawvereERCat` (section 6.1)".
