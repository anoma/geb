## 1. Scope

Sub-phase 6.4 realizes the Lemma 12 bound as computation: a
normalizer on Gödel codes of `1λ(natAlgSig)` terms, constructed as an
ER morphism, whose clocked iteration lands the standard denotation of
a closed source term. It is the computational content behind the
paper's proof of Proposition 13 (Leivant III, journal p. 226): "this
can be performed in time elementary in the size of
`F̄ a^{λτ̄}`" — the sentence the paper leaves to the machine model
and footnote 10 declares model-immaterial.

The construction has three layers, each consuming the previous:

1. **Term level** (generic in `A`): a deterministic evaluator
   `detStep : Tm → Tm` implementing Leivant's rank-elimination
   procedure, with a clock theorem re-derived from the 6.3 induction
   structure (section 4).
2. **Coding level** (`natAlgSig`): `codeTm : Tm → ℕ` over Szudzik
   pairing, the code-size lemma cluster, and word encode/decode
   (section 5).
3. **ER level** (`natAlgSig`): `normStep : ERMor1 1` mirroring
   `detStep` on codes, `normRun : ERMor1 2` by bounded recursion,
   the in-system clock, adequacy, and `collapseER` /
   `collapseER_interp` (section 6).

Deliverables are placed in `GebLean/Ramified/Soundness/` (layer 1
beside the 6.3 detector layer, layers 2–3 in a new file), with tests
under `GebLeanTests/`. Topic branch: `feat/ramified-p6.4-normalizer`.

### 1.1 Non-goals

- **A `KMor1` normalizer definition.** The parent sub-plan's
  interface line `normalizer (a : ℕ) : KMor1 (a + 1)` is not shipped
  as a definition; section 3 records the reconciliation. The K^sim
  landing (gate decision G3) is stated as a corollary through
  `erToK`, not constructed.
- **η-normality.** Unchanged from 6.3 (its decision D3): `Normal`
  means β/ι-normal; `detStep` performs β and ι contractions only.
- **General injectivity of `codeTm`.** Adequacy decodes only word
  codes (section 5.3); a cross-context injectivity theorem has no
  consumer.
- **Confluence.** As in 6.3 (its decision P4), the output is
  identified semantically through the evaluator, not by uniqueness of
  normal forms.
- **The deferred-work ticket** (6.2's and 6.3's consolidated items):
  untouched, one concern per branch, except the two items this design
  explicitly consumes (section 8.3).

## 2. Sources and pinned forms

DOI `10.1016/S0168-0072(98)00040-2` (Leivant, *Ramified recurrence and
computational complexity III: higher type recurrence and elementary
complexity*, APAL 96, 1999). Local PDF page = journal page − 208.

- **Lemma 12** (p. 226): a term of height `h` and redex rank `q`
  reduces to a normal term of height `≤ 2_q(h)` in time
  `O((2_{q+1}(h))²)`. Formalized in 6.3 as `lemma12` with clock
  corollary `lemma12_clock : k ≤ (redexRank t + 1) * tower
  (redexRank t + 1) (Tm.height t)`. The 6.3 chain is an existential
  witness; this sub-phase re-derives the same bound for a
  deterministic iteration (section 4.3).
- **Proposition 13** (p. 226): formalized in 6.3 as
  `prop13_elementary`. Its proof sentence "to calculate `fa` we need
  only normalize `F̄ a^{λτ̄}` ... in time elementary in the size" is
  what `collapseER_interp` realizes as a constructed morphism.
- **Footnote 10** (p. 226): "The computation model is immaterial,
  since polynomial reducibilities are absorbed here by the constant
  coefficient." This licenses D3: the choice among URM, ER
  arithmetic, or any polynomially-related step model does not affect
  the elementary bound; the criterion is proof economy in this
  repository.
- **Section 3.1** (pp. 219–220): register machines over a free
  algebra — the machine model the paper pairs with Lemma 12's cost
  accounting. Cited as the pattern anchor for "a deterministic step
  relation iterated under a clock"; not transcribed.
- **Lemma 6** (p. 221): the clock composition
  `f(a) = [π₁](a, c · 2_q(sz(a)))` — simulate a machine by iterating
  its step function a clock-many times, with the clock a
  tower-of-size expression. The Phase-5 development formalized this
  pattern in the opposite direction (`machineClock`,
  `urm_ramified_definable`); this sub-phase reuses the format
  (section 6.3), not the statements.

Per the transcription-marking rule: every deliverable of this
sub-phase is **novel realization** anchored to the forms above; none
is a transcription of a published statement. The docstrings cite
Lemma 12/Proposition 13 (for the bound and the consumer) and footnote
10 (for the model choice) with the DOI.

## 3. Interface reconciliation

The parent sub-plan §"Sub-phase 6.4 (L5)" lists the deliverable
interface at the level of intent. Two reconciliations, both recorded
here as the mini-sub-plan the gate demanded:

1. **The K^sim landing is a statement, not an artifact.** The parent
   interface names `normalizer (a : ℕ) : KMor1 (a + 1)`
   (`KSimURMSimulator`-style) and obtains the ER landing by
   equivalence transfer. Since the gate was written, the ER side has
   its own clocked-iteration combinators with correctness
   specifications (`ERMor1.boundedRec`,
   `boundedRec_eq_natRec_of_bounded`,
   `ERMor1.simultaneousBoundedRec`), and 6.5 consumes an `ERMorN`.
   Constructing in ER directly removes the transfer step; an
   `erToK`-image definition would have no consumer. Resolution: the
   clocked normalizer is `normRun : ERMor1 2` (section 6.2); G3's
   landing is stated as one corollary (section 6.5) via `erToK` and
   `erToK_interp`, so K^sim_2 definability remains on record. The
   equivalence `erKSimEquiv : LawvereERCat ≌ LawvereKSimDCat 2`
   guarantees nothing is lost by the change of construction site.
2. **A term-level deterministic evaluator now has a consumer.** The
   6.3 design's non-goals recorded "an executable normalizer on `Tm`
   itself would have no consumer". This sub-phase supersedes that
   record: `detStep` (section 4) is the semantic anchor of
   `normStep`, consumed by the commutation lemma (section 6.1). The
   6.3 record was correct for 6.3's scope; the consumer it
   anticipated might never exist now exists.

The remaining parent-interface items map as follows: `codeTm` —
section 5.2; `normStep` — section 6.1; `normalizer_clock` — section
6.2 (`normRun_normal`); `normalizer_adequate` — section 6.4
(`collapseER_interp` is its final form); `collapseER`,
`collapseER_interp` — section 6.4.

## 4. Layer 1: the term-level deterministic normalizer

### 4.1 Selection rule (decision D2)

`detStep : Tm (oneLambdaSig A) Γ s → Tm (oneLambdaSig A) Γ s` is a
total computable function:

- if `betaRedexRank t = q > 0`: contract the innermost rank-`q`
  β-redex selected by child-priority depth-first descent — descend
  into the first child (in argument order) whose subterm carries a
  rank-`q` redex (`betaRedexRank child = q`), and contract at the
  current node only when no child does and the node is itself a
  rank-`q` redex (`topBetaRank = q`);
- else if `hasIota t = true`: contract the ι-redex (destructor or
  case) selected by the same child-priority descent on `hasIota`;
- else (`Normal t`): `detStep t = t`.

Child priority is what makes the selected redex innermost, and
innermost-at-maximal-rank is what the count argument needs: the
selected rank-`q` redex `app' (lam' D) E` contains no rank-`q` redex
properly inside, so its contraction `instantiate₁ E D`:

- creates new redexes only of rank `< q` (a redex created at a
  substitution site has rank `RType.ord σ`, and
  `ord σ < ord (σ → τ) ≤ q` for the contracted redex's arrow sort);
- duplicates only sub-redexes of `E`, none of which has rank `q`.

Hence the number of rank-`q` redexes strictly decreases by one per
step and `betaRedexRank` never increases — the deterministic
counterpart of the 6.3 `beta_cycle` invariant. The ι phase is
size-decreasing exactly as in `iota_normalize`. The rule agrees in
substance with the paper's procedure (Lemma 12's recurrence processes
subterms before the root at each rank).

### 4.2 Definition shape

`detStep` is a `PolyFix.ind` fold requiring no position type: at an
`app`/`lam` node it consults the computable 6.3 detectors on the
children (`betaRedexRank child = q`, `hasIota child`, `topBetaRank`,
`topIota`) and recurses into the first child that carries a target
redex, contracting at the current node only when no child does
(section 4.1's child-priority rule). The same detector-consulting
recursion is what `normStep` mirrors on codes; the detectors'
code-level expressibility (section 6.1) is the reason no separate
position machinery is needed at either level.

The β/ι phase split is read off `betaRedexRank t` at the root of each
call: the rank parameter `q` is threaded down the recursion so that
subterm calls target the same rank the root selected.

### 4.3 Statements

```lean
def detStep : Tm (oneLambdaSig A) Γ s → Tm (oneLambdaSig A) Γ s

theorem detStep_normal (h : Normal t) : detStep t = t

theorem detStep_sound (h : ¬ Normal t) : OneLambdaStep t (detStep t)

def detIter (n : ℕ) : Tm (oneLambdaSig A) Γ s → Tm (oneLambdaSig A) Γ s

theorem detIter_normal (t : Tm (oneLambdaSig A) Γ s) :
    Normal (detIter ((redexRank t + 1) * tower (redexRank t + 1) (Tm.height t)) t)
```

together with the chain corollaries the ER layer consumes:
`detIter_reduces : ReflTransGen OneLambdaStep t (detIter n t)` (each
non-identity step is an `OneLambdaStep` by `detStep_sound`, so
`oneEval_reduces` applies to the iteration), and stability
`detIter_normal_stable : Normal t → detIter n t = t`.

`detIter_normal`'s bound is the `lemma12_clock` shape re-derived for
the deterministic chain. The re-derivation reuses the 6.3 bounding
lemmas as-is (`betaRedexRank_instantiate₁_le`, the `stepWithin` size
ceilings, `size_le_pow_height`, the tower absorption lemmas); the
induction is over the lexicographic measure (current β-rank, count of
maximal-rank β-redexes, size for the ι phase). If the deterministic
accounting yields a cheaper constant than `(q+1) · 2_{q+1}(h)`, the
statement still fixes this bound for interface stability with
`lemma12_clock`.

Expected difficulty concentration: `detStep_sound` re-runs the
inversion content of 6.3's step-existence lemmas
(`exists_iota_step_of_hasIota` was 6.3's escalated item) in
deterministic form, and absorbs the deferred
`exists_beta_step_of_topBetaRank` shape (section 8.3).

### 4.4 Placement and genericity (decision D6)

Layer 1 is generic in `A` with the `[Fintype A.B] [LinearOrder A.B]`
ambient, placed beside the detector layer — in
`GebLean/Ramified/Soundness/Normalization.lean` if its charter and

## 5. Layer 2: the coding level

All of layer 2 is pinned at `natAlgSig` (decision D6): the only
consumer varies neither the algebra nor the signature, and the
`natAlgSig` constants (`numCtors = 2`, arities 0 and 1) simplify the
op-tag payloads.

### 5.1 Sort codes

```lean
def codeRType : RType → ℕ
```

A three-shape `PolyFix.ind` fold over `rTypeSig`:
`o ↦ Nat.pair 0 0`, `arrow σ τ ↦ Nat.pair 1 (Nat.pair (codeRType σ)
(codeRType τ))`, `omega τ ↦ Nat.pair 2 (codeRType τ)` (present for
totality; `1λ(A)` sorts are `IsSimple`, so `omega` does not occur in
codes the normalizer reads). Companion `ordCode : ℕ → ℕ` mirroring
`RType.ord`, with `ordCode_codeRType : ordCode (codeRType σ) =
RType.ord σ` — the lemma through which rank reads on codes are
discharged.

### 5.2 Term codes (decision D1)

```lean
def codeTm {Γ s} : Tm (oneLambdaSig natAlgSig) Γ s → ℕ
```

The minimal sorted code, by `PolyFix.ind`:

- variable leaf: `Nat.pair 0 i` (the de Bruijn index `i`; the sort
  proof is not encoded — contexts are threaded top-down by every
  consumer, never stored);
- op node: `Nat.pair 1 (Nat.pair opCode childrenPack)`, where
  `opCode` is `app σ τ ↦ Nat.pair 0 (Nat.pair (codeRType σ)
  (codeRType τ))`, `lam σ τ ↦ Nat.pair 1 (Nat.pair (codeRType σ)
  (codeRType τ))`, `con b ↦ Nat.pair 2 (ctor position of b)`,
  `dstr j ↦ Nat.pair 3 j`, `case ↦ Nat.pair 4 0`, and
  `childrenPack` packs the zero, one, or two child codes by pairing.

Sorts appear exactly where the ops carry them (`app`/`lam` embed two
`RType` trees; this is forced data, per the survey of
`oneLambdaSig`). Result sorts and contexts are functions of the op
tag and the ambient index, hence never stored.

### 5.3 Word encode and decode

```lean
def codeConc : ℕ → ℕ      -- n ↦ codeTm (conc (word n))
def decodeWord : ℕ → ℕ    -- con-spine code ↦ its numeral read-off

theorem decodeWord_codeConc (n : ℕ) : decodeWord (codeConc n) = n
```

`decodeWord` is a don't-care on non-word codes. No general
`codeTm`-injectivity is stated: adequacy (section 6.4) identifies the
output semantically (`oneEval` invariance ending at
`normal_closed_o_eq_conc`, which forces the closed normal sort-`o`
term to be a `conc` word), so the only decoding performed is on the
word fragment, where `decodeWord_codeConc` suffices.

### 5.4 The code-size cluster (decision D4, term-to-code bridge)

Two lemma families make the bounded-recursion trace bound of section
6.2 dischargeable:

1. **Growth.** From `Nat.pair_le_sq`
   (`Utilities/ComputationalComplexity.lean`): pairing depth is term
   height and each level at most squares the payload sum, so
   `codeTm t ≤ tower 2 (c * (Tm.size t + sortPayload t))` for a
   constant `c`, where `sortPayload` bounds the `codeRType` values
   occurring in `t`. (The exact statement shape — one lemma or a
   small family threading `size`, `height`, and the sort payload —
   is left to the plan; the tower-2 envelope is the fixed point.)
2. **Sort stability along reduction.** β and ι contraction introduce
   no sort absent from the source term (`instantiate₁` and the
   `dstr`/`case` reducts reuse existing sorts), so the maximal
   `codeRType` payload along a `detStep` chain is a constant of the
   source. Combined with the 6.3 `stepWithin` size ceilings, every
   code along the chain from `t` is bounded by a monotone
   tower-shaped function of `Tm.size t`, `Tm.height t`, and
   `sortPayload t` — the `h`/`h_mono` hypotheses of
   `boundedRec_eq_natRec_of_bounded`.

## 6. Layer 3: the ER realization

### 6.1 `normStep` and the commutation lemma

Structural recursion over a Szudzik-coded tree is re-expressed as
`ERMor1.boundedRec` recursion on a numeric bound, following the
existing precedents (`LawvereNatBTV2.lean`'s tree folds,
`LawvereTreeERArith.lean`'s `treeFoldOnCode`/`mutuFoldOnCode`) —
adapted, not duplicated, where shapes permit. Dispatch on kind bits
and op tags is nested `ERMor1.condN` with the `LawvereERBool` atoms;
reads are `natUnpairFst`/`natUnpairSnd`. The code-level detectors
(`ordCode` and the mirrors of `betaRedexRank`, `hasIota`, the
`Normal` test) are folds in the same style.

```lean
def normStep : ERMor1 1

theorem normStep_mirror {s} (t : Tm (oneLambdaSig natAlgSig) [] s) :
    normStep.interp ![codeTm t] = codeTm (detStep t)
```

The mirror is stated on the image of `codeTm` at closed terms (the
consumer's generality; if the proof naturally supports open terms
with a threaded context argument, the plan may widen it); off-image
behavior is unspecified. This cluster — one lemma per dispatch arm,
including a code-level `instantiate₁` (de Bruijn shifting on codes)
with its own small fusion family mirroring the `Binding` substitution
laws — is the largest proof obligation of the sub-phase.

### 6.2 `normRun` and the clock statement

```lean
def normRun : ERMor1 2   -- arguments: clock, code

theorem normRun_interp {s} (k : ℕ) (t : Tm (oneLambdaSig natAlgSig) [] s) :
    normRun.interp ![k, codeTm t] = codeTm (detIter k t)

theorem normRun_normal (t : Tm (oneLambdaSig natAlgSig) [] s) :
    Normal (detIter ((redexRank t + 1) * tower (redexRank t + 1) (Tm.height t)) t) ∧
    normRun.interp ![(redexRank t + 1) * tower (redexRank t + 1) (Tm.height t), codeTm t]
      = codeTm (detIter ((redexRank t + 1) * tower (redexRank t + 1) (Tm.height t)) t)
```

(`normRun_normal` is the parent interface's `normalizer_clock`; its
exact packaging — one conjunction or two statements — is left to the
plan.) `normRun` is `boundedRec` over `normStep` with the clock in
the recursion-variable slot; the trace bound is discharged by the
section 5.4 cluster through `boundedRec_eq_natRec_of_bounded`. The
clock value is already in the Phase-5 `c · 2_q` format
(`c = q + 1`, tower height `q + 1`, argument `h`);
`tower_add_le_tower` and `clock_mono` (`Definability/Bounds.lean`)
handle any offset reshaping the proofs need.

### 6.3 The in-system clock and code builder

For a fixed closed source `F : Tm (rlmrOSig natAlgSig) []
(RType.arrow (RType.omega τ) RType.o)` (the `prop13_elementary`
scenario), with
meta-level constants `q_F` (rank ceiling of the applied translate)
and `h_F` (height offset), both computed from `barTm F`:

```lean
def buildCode (F …) : ERMor1 1   -- v ↦ codeTm (app' F̄ (conc (word v)))
def clockER (F …) : ERMor1 1     -- v ↦ (q_F + 1) * tower (q_F + 1) (h_F + v + c)
```

`buildCode` composes the fixed constant `codeTm (barTm F)` with
`codeConc` (a `boundedRec` fold) and the code-level application
pairer. `clockER`'s tower height is a fixed meta-level constant, so
the expression is a `(q_F + 1)`-fold composition of the ER
exponential (`bprod`-derived `2^x`) with the multiplication and
successor atoms; input-dependence enters only through the tower
argument. Rank and height facts making `q_F`/`h_F` correct
(`redexRank (app' F̄ (conc a)) ≤ q_F` and
`Tm.height (app' F̄ (conc a)) ≤ h_F + …` for every word `a`) are
lemmas of this section, discharged from the 6.3 measure equations
(`redexRank_app'`, the `height` node equations, `normal_conc`).

The unary case is stated first; the `a`-ary input form reuses the
same components with the Phase-5 precedent for common-clock
domination (`maxOfNat_le_sum_succ`-style) where a maximum over inputs
is inconvenient.

### 6.4 `collapseER` and adequacy

```lean
def collapseER (F …) : ERMorN a 1
  -- decodeWord ∘ normRun ∘ (clockER, buildCode)

theorem collapseER_interp (F …) (v : Fin a → ℕ) :
    (collapseER F).interp v = …   -- the standard denotation of F at v
```

`collapseER` is the atomic builder for a single codomain-`o` source
morphism (output arity `1`); the 6.5b `collapseFunctor` obtains its
general morphism map by applying `collapseER` to each of the
`objLen Δ` codomain components and tupling into
`ERMorN (objLen Γ) (objLen Δ)` (tupling is definitional for
`ERMorN`), with a tupling lemma lifting the per-component
`collapseER_interp` to the parent's whole-morphism
`collapseDenotation g`; that tupling layer is 6.5 scope.
`collapseER_interp` is the parent interface's `normalizer_adequate`
in final form and the 6.5b input. Proof chain: `normRun_interp` and
`normRun_normal` land the code of a normal term; `detIter_reduces`
plus `oneEval_reduces` carry the standard denotation along the chain;
`normal_closed_o_eq_conc` forces the normal form to be a `conc` word;
`decodeWord_codeConc` reads it off; the identification of the word
with the denotation of `F` follows the `prop13_elementary` pattern
(`appEval_sourceWord`, `prop11_represents` — the decision-P4 dividend
recorded in the parent plan). The right-hand side of
`collapseER_interp` is fixed by 6.5a's `collapseDenotation` consumer;
the plan pins its exact spelling against
`Definability/Top.lean`'s `ramifiedDenotation` conventions.

### 6.5 The K-side corollary (decision D3)

One theorem, no new definition:

```lean
theorem collapseER_ksim_definable (F …) :
    ∃ g : KMorN a 1, (∀ v, KMorN.interp g v = (collapseER F).interp v)
```

realized by `erToKN` and `erToKN_interp` (statement shape indicative;
the plan aligns it with the `LawvereERKSim` conventions). This states
gate decision G3's K^sim_2 landing without shipping a consumer-less
definition; `erKSimEquiv` supplies the categorical form if a later
phase wants it.

## 7. Acceptance

Per the recorded testing cautions (no `#guard`/kernel reduction on ER
interps or `PolyFix` terms; evaluate through proven `@[simp]` lemmas
and node equations):

- **Layer 1**: `detStep` on a hand-built β-redex and a hand-built
  ι-redex (the 6.3 acceptance instances give templates, e.g. the
  `beta_cycle` `q = 1` example); `detStep_normal` on `conc`;
  `detIter` reaching a `Normal` term on a small instance, asserted
  through the lemmas.
- **Layer 2**: `codeRType`/`codeTm` node equations by `rfl`/`simp`;
  `decodeWord_codeConc` at small numerals through the equations.
- **Layer 3**: `normStep_mirror` instantiated on a small redex;
  end-to-end `collapseER_interp` on a 6.2-precedent acceptance term
  (the constant-zero abstraction used by 6.3.12, or the doubling
  morphism if available at this sort), asserted via the lemma chain,
  not kernel evaluation.

## 8. Process

### 8.1 Workflow

The 6.2/6.3 pattern: this design is adversarially reviewed to
convergence, then user-reviewed; the implementation plan
(`superpowers:writing-plans`) follows the same two gates; execution
is subagent-driven with per-task two-verdict reviews and a
whole-branch review before the push-review handoff. Every `.lean`
commit passes the pre-commit triad; the capstones are
`lean_verify`-checked (`propext`, `Classical.choice`, `Quot.sound`
only). All new declarations live in `GebLean.*` namespaces
(`topNamespace` linter; the nolints list is not extended).
