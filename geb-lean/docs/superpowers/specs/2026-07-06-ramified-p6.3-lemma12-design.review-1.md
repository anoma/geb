### Adjudication of the mathematical checks

- Constructor-word consequence (§4.3). For `oneLambdaSig`, a closed
  β/destructor/case-normal term of type `o` is an application spine
  whose head is `var` (excluded by closedness), `lam` (impossible at
  type `o` unapplied; a β-redex if applied), or a constant. A `con`
  head is saturated by typing (partial application has arrow type),
  and its arguments are closed normal of type `o`, hence constructor
  words by strong induction on size. A `dstr` head over a
  constructor word matches `dstrHit` or `dstrMiss` (the guards
  `j < ar i` and `ar i ≤ j` are exhaustive) — a redex, excluded. A
  `case` head at type `o` is saturated by typing, and its scrutinee
  is a constructor word by induction, so `OneLambdaStep.case`
  applies — excluded. Arrow-typed terms cannot occupy argument
  positions of the constants (all constant argument sorts are `o`),
  so no partially-applied constant hides inside. The argument is
  sound for this signature.
- Stage-4 accounting (§6). Against the actual rules: `dstrHit`
  yields `a j`, `dstrMiss` yields the scrutinee `c_i ā`, `case`
  yields the branch `b idx` — each a proper subterm of the redex,
  so size strictly decreases. The contractum has type `o` and a
  type-`o` node never occupies the function child of an `app`
  (which has arrow type), and the contractum is a pre-existing
  subterm of a β-normal term, so no new β-redex arises — the
  source's parenthetical, correctly transcribed. A contraction can
  create a new η-contractum (removing the sole occurrence of a
  bound variable from an application body) and new ι-redexes; both
  are harmless — `Normal` excludes η by definition D3, and the
  strong induction on size absorbs new ι-redexes.
- Rank-1 ι subtlety (§4.2). Under the p. 224 definition every
  destructor or case redex has rank 1 (the applied subterm has type
  `o → o`), and every β-redex has rank ≥ 1 (an abstraction has
  arrow type). Hence for a term whose β-redex rank is `q ≥ 2` the
  two notions agree; a β-normal term with ι-redexes has p. 224 rank
  exactly 1 against internal β-rank 0. The spec's "agree up to the
  `q = 0` and `q = 1` edge cases" is exact, and deferring the edge
  cases to the plan step is coherent.

### Major

#### R1-M1 — Lemma 12's calculus: §1 and §2 name different systems

Section 2 pins the paper's statement verbatim: "If `F` is a term of
`RλMR_o^ω(A)` … then `F` reduces in `RλMR_o^ω(A)` …" (confirmed
against p. 226). Section 1 restates the lemma as "a term of
`1λ(A)`", and the §5.1 Lean statement is over
`Tm (oneLambdaSig A)`. The `1λ(A)` reading is almost certainly the
correct one — p. 224 defines redex, normal, rank, and redex rank
"for `1λ(A)`", the proof opens by viewing `F` as a pure-`1λ` term
and closes with "a normal term of `1λ(A)` is obtained", and
Proposition 13 applies Lemma 12 to `F̄ a^{λτ̄}`, a `1λ(A)` term —
so the paper's statement letters appear to be an erratum. But the
document nowhere records the divergence: the pinned form and the
formalized statement silently name different calculi. Under the
transcription discipline, add one sentence to the §2 Lemma 12
bullet recording that the statement's `RλMR_o^ω(A)` is read as
`1λ(A)`, with the reason (p. 224 terminology scope; the proof's
first and last sentences; Proposition 13's use), and align §1's
summary with the pinned quote plus that recorded reading.

#### R1-M2 — the `c · size` cost is misattributed

Section 2, proof paragraph (ii), pins: "the elimination of rank-`q`
redexes in a term costs time `≤ c · size(F^q)`". The paper (p. 226)
charges that cost to the single top-level contraction, not to the
whole elimination pass: "… then `F^q` is taken to be `[E^q/x]D^q`.
The height of `F^q` is ≤ height(D^q) + height(E^q), and this single
reduction, which consists in copying over `E^q` at most
`size(D^q)` times, and copying `D^q` once, takes time
`≤ c size(F^q) = c 2_1(height(F^q)) = c 2^{2^h}`". The recurrence
on `F` performs one such contraction per recursive position, so the
per-cycle total is not literally `c · size(F^q)`; the paper leaves
the aggregation to the displayed cycle bounds ("The next cycle …
in time `O(2_3(h))`. The total computation time of `F^o` from `F`
is thus `O(2_{q+1}(h))`"). Since the plan step will derive the
stage-2 cost recurrence from this pinned form, the misattribution
propagates. Fix: restate the pinned sentence as the paper has it
(single top-level contraction `≤ c · size(F^q)`), and let the
per-cycle aggregation be an explicit plan-step obligation rather
than an attribute of the source.

#### R1-M3 — §5.2 statement shape names a nonexistent type and overstates generality

The `prop13_elementary` shape quantifies `{A τ}` and writes
`∀ a : FreeAlgebra A`. Two defects: (a) the repository's free
algebra is `FreeAlg` (`GebLean/Ramified/AlgSig.lean`); `FreeAlgebra`
is mathlib's free `R`-algebra, a different object; (b) the anchors
the same subsection names — `barTm`, `bbRep`, `prop11_represents`
(`GebLean/Ramified/Soundness/{BarRep,Represents}.lean`) — are all
specialized to `natAlgSig` (`Tm (rlmrOSig natAlgSig)`), so the
`{A}`-generic statement cannot be anchored as described. Fix:
write the shape at `natAlgSig` with `FreeAlg natAlgSig` (matching
the anchors), or record generalization of the 6.1/6.2 layer as an
explicit prerequisite with its own reason.

### Minor

#### R1-m1 — the intersection relation leaves the initial term unconstrained

In the §5.1 shape, the size bound sits inside the step relation
(`OneLambdaStep a b ∧ Tm.size b ≤ S …`), so it constrains the
target of each step. Every chain element except `t` itself is some
step's target, so intermediates and the final term are bounded —
the device does express the invariant — but `size t` is not
bounded by the statement. The gap is benign (`oneLambdaSig` nodes
have at most two children, so `size t` is bounded by an exponential
in `height t`, which any clock-format `S` dominates), but the spec
presents the device as "a size invariant on the intermediate
terms" without recording the initial-term exclusion. Record it, and
either note that `S` dominates the initial size by construction or
add the conjunct `Tm.size t ≤ S …`; this is exactly the sort of
edge the §8 plan-step item ("the intersection-relation device …
or its replacement") should inherit explicitly.

#### R1-m2 — "6.2's `Reduces`" does not exist as a named definition

Section 5.1 says `RelatesInSteps` "carries bridges to the
`Relation.ReflTransGen` form that 6.2's `Reduces` uses". The
Ramified tree defines no `Reduces`; the 6.2 code uses
`Relation.ReflTransGen OneLambdaStep` directly (`OneLambda.lean`,
`Represents.lean`). `Reduces` is the parent sub-plan's sketch name
only. Say "the `Relation.ReflTransGen OneLambdaStep` form the 6.2
development uses" (the bridge claim itself is accurate:
`RelatesInSteps.reflTransGen` / `ReflTransGen.relatesInSteps`).

#### R1-m3 — section 5's page range

Section 1 cites "Leivant III section 5 (journal pp. 226-227)"
(inherited from the parent sub-plan). Section 5 lies entirely on
p. 226; p. 227 opens section 6 (Theorem 14). Cite p. 226. The §2
per-item citations are already correct.

#### R1-m4 — "baked into" / "bakes in"

Sections 1.1 and 5.2 use "baked into" / "bakes in" for the Lemma 4

### Nit

#### R1-n1 — "fire" inside a pinned form

Section 2, paragraph (iii): "at most `size(F^o)` of them fire".
The paper: "at most size(F^o) of them can be performed". Within a
pinned form, use the source's verb.

#### R1-n2 — unmarked "strictly" gloss

Section 2, paragraph (iii) writes "these strictly decrease term
size" where the paper has "these reductions decrease term size".
The strengthening is correct (each contractum is a proper subterm;
the `≤ size(F^o)` count needs it) but is an unmarked addition
inside a quoted-structure bullet; mark it as a gloss or move it to
§6 stage 4, which already carries the parenthetical justification.

#### R1-n3 — `normalizeSteps` disposition unrecorded

The parent sub-plan's interface sketch bounds `normalizeSteps t`;
the §5.1 shape replaces it with the existential step count `k` of
the counted chain. This is within the gate's remit (D2 fixes the
cost model), but §5.3's interface-conformance list is silent on
it; one sentence recording the subsumption closes the loop.

#### R1-n4 — the Theorem 14 consumer claim is forward-looking

Non-goal 1 asserts that "Theorem 14 through Phase 7" produces terms
of exactly the shape `Ωτ → o`. Theorem 14 (p. 227) includes
condition (5) "f is represented in `1λ(A)`", whose Lean shape
Phase 7 has not yet fixed; the assertion is a prediction about a
future statement, not a verifiable fact about the repository.
Acceptable as a recorded reason, but phrase it as the intended
Phase 7 statement shape (or add the caveat) so the non-goal does
not read as an established property.
