## Correction 5 (the budget slot) — the durable record

Discovered at Task 6.4.13, adjudicated in-session, user-ratified,
and reconciled into the 6.4 spec (§1, §6.2, §6.3, §6.4) and the 6.4
plan (Tasks 6.4.13, 6.4.15) by this branch's amendment commit:

- The originally specified `normRun : ERMor1 2` with an unconditional
  interpretation lemma is not ER-realizable — not over arbitrary
  codes and not over all closed terms. The chain ceiling `codeCeil t`
  has tower height growing with `redexRank t` (through `detClock`),
  and rank is unbounded over closed terms, so no fixed elementary
  bound morphism dominates the iteration trace uniformly. This is the
  classical non-elementarity of unbounded-rank normalization; the
  paper's Proposition 13 is per-fixed-`F` precisely for this reason.
- The repair: the ceiling is an input. `normRun : ERMor1 3` with a
  budget slot; `normRun_interp_of_le` conditional on trace
  domination; closed-term statements instantiate the budget at
  `codeCeil t`; the collapse assemblies feed the per-`F` in-system
  budgets (`budgetER` / `budgetERN`), whose tower heights are
  constants computed from `q_F`.
- Declined alternative (recorded in case a future consumer appears):
  a code-intrinsic size-potential envelope giving a fixed-height
  bound of shape `tower (4, k + n)` for `stepCode` iteration on
  arbitrary codes — plausible, but a substantial cluster with no
  consumer today.
- Standing consequence: any future work wanting a rank-uniform
  internal normalizer in ER (self-interpreters, reflection) is
  impossible; such work must thread rank or budget as an explicit
  resource, or move to a stronger theory (a Gödel-T-level normalizer
  would be uniform).
