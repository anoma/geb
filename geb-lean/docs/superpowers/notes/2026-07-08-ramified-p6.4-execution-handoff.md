## Plan corrections recorded during execution

Four defects in the plan (not in the spec's decisions and not in any
landed theorem) were found and corrected during execution; each
correction was verified by an independent per-task reviewer, and every
binding statement survived intact or strictly stronger. Items 2-4
share one root cause the plan's note N3 misunderstood: `codeTm` codes
variables at absolute context positions (de Bruijn levels).

1. **Input representation (spec correction, pre-execution, commit
   `0be2bff3`)**: `prop13_elementary` applies `barTm F` to
   `bbRep a (barTy τ)`, not to a `conc` word; `codeBbRep` added, the
   builder repointed.
2. **Envelope argument (Task 6.4.8)**: the plan's literal bound
   omitted the context length and is false for open terms (a
   variable's code carries its index, bounded only by `Γ.length`).
   Landed: `codeTm t ≤ tower 2 (6 * (2*Tm.size t + sortPayload t +
   Γ.length + 1))`; `Γ.length` is definitionally constant along
   `detIter`, so the chain ceiling closes; size weight 2 is forced and
   tight (lam-node margin).
3. **Substituend shifting (Task 6.4.9)**: N3's no-shift claim is false
   for substituends containing binders (their bound variables shift
   under weakening; verified numerically). Fix: `shiftCode` applied at
   each `lam` descent inside `subCode`; the binding `subCode_codeTm`
   unchanged.
4. **Context-generic step (Task 6.4.11)**: the plan's generic-`Γ`
   `stepCode_codeTm` is provably false for any bare `ℕ → ℕ` (the
   contraction cut depends on `Γ.length`; reviewer-verified
   counterexample). Landed: level-threaded `stepCodeAt` with the
   generic mirror at `j = Γ.length` — which is the spec §6.1's own
   form; the plan had over-widened — plus clamped `stepCode` with the
   closed-term commutation. Propagation: Task 6.4.13's
   `normRun_codeTm`/`normRun_normal` are to be stated at closed terms
   (their sole consumer, `collapseER`, is closed).

These four corrections await user ratification at push review,
together with the deferred-ticket additions noted below.

## The Task 6.4.12 escalation

The dispatched implementer (most capable tier) performed the plan's
mandated re-checkpoint on `ERMor1.foldBTLOnCode`
(`GebLean/Utilities/ERTreeArith.lean:615`) and returned BLOCKED with a
decomposition case, committing nothing:

- `foldBTLOnCode` is not instantiable for `stepCode`: it hardwires the
  binary-tree even/odd node dispatch, while every `stepCode`
  ingredient dispatches on the kind bit and operation-kind bit at
  different unpair depths. A fresh generic course-of-values combinator
  over Szudzik-coded trees (roughly 350-400 lines including a
  correctness lemma parameterized by a client node-faithfulness
  hypothesis) must be built first — properly homed in
  `GebLean/Utilities/` beside `foldBTLOnCode` as the missing general
  concept.
- The level/substituend threading (correction 4 above: `j → j + 1`
  and `e → shiftCode j e` under binders) means `stepCodeAt` is not a
  single course-of-values fold on the code alone; the recursion tables
  must be keyed by `Nat.pair depth code`.
- Estimated shape: about 12 dependency-ordered waves (the generic
  combinator; the non-recursive reads; seven one-dimensional folds
  with tower-2 majorants; a gated two-dimensional variant for the
  level-threaded pieces; the substitution and β-worker realizations;
  `stepBound` in ER; the dispatch-and-clamp assembly) — multi-thousand
  lines, comparable to the Proposition 11 effort, which was executed
  as roughly twenty sub-briefs.
- Provability is not in question: the clamp in `stepCode`'s definition
  makes the binding `normStep_interp (n) : normStep.interp ![n] =
  stepCode n` achievable for all `n`; the conditional fallback was
  explicitly not adopted. The obstacle is volume.

The full adaptation note (table layouts, bound expressions, the

Question raised at the gate: did the formalization diverge from
Leivant III — did we prove something weaker than the paper, blocking
later results? Answer: no.

1. What the paper proves, this workstream proved, pinned and reviewed:
   Lemma 12 and Proposition 13 landed in sub-phase 6.3 with statements
   reviewed against the pinned source forms (`lemma12`,
   `prop13_elementary`, both axiom-clean). Where 6.3's accounting
   deviates it deviates downward (its decision P2: the project's step
   relation substitutes in one step, so the step bound is smaller than
   the paper's `O((2_{q+1}(h))²)` — freedom footnote 10 grants). This
   branch's `detIter_normal` is stronger than the paper's Lemma 12,
   not weaker: the paper's chain is existential; the same clock is
   proved here for a specific deterministic strategy.
2. The paper contains no argument for 6.4 to transcribe. Proposition
   13's proof ends with "this can be performed in time elementary in
   the size", and footnote 10 (p. 226) declares the computation model
   immaterial, "since polynomial reducibilities are absorbed here by
   the constant coefficient". The paper never constructs a normalizer
   in any formal machine model. Sub-phase 6.4 is marked realization,
   not transcription (spec decision D5): the landing normalizer exists
   because the Phase-7 goal (a faithful `collapseFunctor` into
   `LawvereERCat`) requires an actual ER morphism — the master plan's
   internal-anchor strategy, not a claim of Leivant's.
3. The escalated volume is footnote 10 paid formally: the copying and
   dispatch costs the paper absorbs into "the constant coefficient"
   are exactly the coding tables, the course-of-values combinator, and
   the per-arm dispatch of the wave recipe. Everything remains
   elementary; the absorption goes through; it is not free in a formal
   system.
4. The four plan corrections touched only the coding layer, which
   exists only in the realization; no landed theorem weakened.
5. Nothing landed blocks 6.5 or Phase 7 mathematically; the gate is
   effort, not provability.
