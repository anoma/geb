**Goal:** Realise the recurrence history code `histCode init step A n` as an
`Era` arithmetic term `eraHistCode` with a proven `eval` lemma, completing
the dominant obligation of Phase 6 (the parent plan's Task 6.4). This
unblocks `eraBSum`/`eraBProd` (Tasks 6.5-6.6) and the capstones (Phase 7).

**Architecture:** Transcribe the hypercube term-realisation of a Diophantine
solution count (Prunescu-Sauras-Altuzarra, arXiv:2407.12928, Section 4) and
its application to the recurrence system `E₂` (Istrate-Prunescu-Shunia,
arXiv:2606.09336, Claims 1-5, p. 9-10). The ℕ-level identity
`histCode = solCount` is already proven (`EraRecurrence.lean`,
`solCount_eq_histCode`). This sub-plan: (a) builds `G₁`/`G₂` as `Era` terms
(prerequisite the parent plan assumed present); (b) certifies the
`diophOf`-encoded predicate is a simple-exponential-polynomial of
coordinate-degree ≤ 2 (Cor 3.6; route (ii) — the `diophOf` atoms keep
`polyExp ≡ 0`, so the full Lemma 3.5 chain reduction is not needed);
(c) builds a reusable `eraCount` count-term combinator (`packM` via the
cube-sum factorisation, then the `HW/w − tᵏ` read-off); (d) applies it to
the inner per-`j` predicate to realise the hit-count as a term
(`eraHitCount`, Claim 3); (e) applies it to `E₂ = diophOf eraHitCount` (the
Lemma-2 encoding of `eraHitCount(ω₁+ω₂+1) = n`) and bridges its zero-count
to `solCount` (via `diophOf`'s unique-witness), giving `eraHistCode`. The
two Section-4 counts are sequential, separated by `diophOf` — see the
"corrected construction" note for why this is a linear pipeline, not a
nested cube.

**Tech stack:** Lean 4, Mathlib (pin `v4.29.0-rc6`), `lake build` /
`lake test` / `lake lint`, `scripts/check-axioms.sh`. Constructive-only (no
`noncomputable`); axiom-clean (`propext`, `Quot.sound`, `Classical.choice`).

## Task 6.4b: Lemma 3.5 SEP reduction to degree 2 (GATE)

**Files:**

- Create: `GebLean/Utilities/EraSepReduce.lean`

> **Route chosen: (ii) certificate, confirmed by the adversarial review
> inspection.** Corollary 3.6 needs only coordinate-degree ≤ 2. The
> adversarial review of this plan inspected the `diophOf` combinators and
> found that **every `diophOf`-reachable `SimpleMonomial` already has
> `polyExp ≡ 0`** (`SimpleMonomial.var`/`diophZero`/`diophSucc`/`diophAdd`/
> `diophPow2` all use `polyExp := fun _ => 0`); the **only** degree-raising
> atom is `diophMul`, via `SimpleMonomial.mulVars j k` (value `ρ j * ρ k`, a
> product of two *distinct* witness slots — degree 2, never a power). So
> after `SosTerm.sqDist` squaring, every cube-coordinate exponent is ≤ 2.
> Therefore the full Lemma 3.5 chain-variable reduction is **not** needed —
> only a *certificate* that the `E₂` predicate's monomials have per-coordinate
> degree ≤ 2. **Safeguard:** if, while building 6.4c, a `diophOf` atom is
> encountered that populates `polyExp` above `0` or squares a *same-slot*
> product (degree > 2 per coordinate), STOP and report — the certificate
> would then fail and route (i) (full Lemma 3.5) would be forced, warranting
> its own sub-plan. Per the inspection this safeguard will not fire.

- [ ] **Step 1: prove the degree-≤2 certificate.** State and prove that every
  `SimpleMonomial` in `(diophOf t).sys` has `polyExp i ≤ 1` for all `i`
  (structural induction on `diophOf`'s combinators / the `SimpleMonomial`
  constructors). Record the `var`/`mulVars` evidence above in the module
  docstring's implementation notes.

- [ ] **Step 2: state and prove the SEP monomial expansion.** The deliverable
  is a predicate-level statement usable by 6.4e: for the `E₂` predicate `P`,
  `δ(P, w)` (as an ℕ-function of the cube point) expands to a finite sum of
  separable monomials `∏ᵢ aᵢ^{uᵢ}·vᵢ^{aᵢ}` with each `uᵢ ≤ 2`. State it as
  the existence of such a monomial list together with the equation
  `δ(P ā, w) · 2^(2w·v(ā)) = Σ (monomials)` pointwise on the cube. The exact
  signature is finalised here against `pE2`'s shape (6.4c); it is the contract
  6.4e consumes. **Dependency direction (resolve the apparent circularity):**
  the pointwise equation is conditioned on the cube-corner bound `w` from
  6.4e Step 1 — `sqDist`'s `(P−Q)²` uses ℕ truncated subtraction, which
  expands to a clean monomial sum only where the residual `P−Q` is
  sign-definite, i.e. on `cubePoints m t` under the `w`-bound. So 6.4e Step 1
  (the `t`/`w` terms and `pE2 ā < 2^w` on the cube) is a **prerequisite** of
  this expansion lemma; state the expansion lemma with the cube-membership
  and bound hypotheses explicit, and order execution 6.4e-Step-1 before
  6.4b-Step-2 (the `t`/`w` ℕ-values are defined in 6.4e but consumed here).

## The corrected construction: a linear pipeline, not a nested cube

> **Revision (2026-06-16, after the E₁/E₂ investigation).** The earlier
> draft of 6.4c-6.4f modelled `pE2` as a single recurrence-instance system
> and counted it directly. That is **wrong**: a single-instance witness cube
> counts `Σ_{(ω₁,ω₂)} hitCount(ω₁+ω₂+1)`, not `solCount = #{(ω₁,ω₂) :
> hitCount = n}`. arXiv:2606.09336 Theorem 2's proof avoids this with a
> **linear pipeline** (Claims 3-5, p. 9-10): (1) the inner per-`j` predicate
> `E` is counted by Section 4 into the hit-count `G` realised **as a term**
> (Claim 3); (2) `G(x) = n` is `diophOf`-encoded (Lemma 2) into a simple SoS
> `E₁`; (3) the linear substitution `x = ω₁+ω₂+1` gives `E₂`; (4) one outer
> Section-4 count on `E₂` yields `H = histCode`. Two **sequential** Section-4
> counts separated by `diophOf` — never a count nested inside a count's
> polynomial. The crux the investigation flagged (does `G`'s term survive the
> degree certificate?) is already discharged: `eraHitCount` is an `ETm`, and
> `diophOf_polyExpZero` (Task 6.4b) holds for **every** `ETm`, so
> `diophOf eraHitCount` is automatically degree-≤2 certified.

The corrected tasks factor a reusable count-term combinator (`eraCount`,
6.4c) applied twice: to the inner per-`j` predicate (giving `eraHitCount`,
6.4d) and to `E₂` (giving `eraHistCode`, 6.4e).
