## Decisions recorded by this mini-sub-plan (for adversarial + user review)

The spec's D1-D5 stand. The plan adds:

### Decision P1 — `RType.ord` totalizes with `ord (Ω τ) = ord τ`

Leivant defines `ord` on simple types only (spec §2, p. 213). `RType.ord` is
total on `RType` for definability by the standard recursor; the `Omega`
clause is chosen as `ord (Ω τ) = ord τ`. Reason: `1λ(A)` terms have simple
sorts (`RType.IsSimple`), so no statement of this development depends on the
clause; delegation to the argument is the smallest total extension and keeps
`ord` monotone under `barTy`-adjacent comparisons should a later phase want
them. The simple-type clauses are the transcription; the `Omega` clause is
novel packaging.

### Decision P2 — exact bound shapes

With `tower` from `GebLean/Utilities/Tower.lean` (`tower 0 x = x`,
`tower (k+1) x = 2 ^ tower k x` — exactly the paper's `2_k(x)`), and writing
`q := redexRank t`, `h := Tm.height t`:

- Height bound `H q h := tower q h` — the paper's `2_q(h)` verbatim, no
  slack.
- Step bound `B q h := q * tower q h + tower (q + 1) h` — cycle `i` performs
  at most `tower i h` β-steps (one step per contraction; the project's step
  relation substitutes in one step, so footnote 10's model freedom makes this
  smaller than the paper's `O((2_{q+1}(h))^2)`); the ι-phase performs at most
  `tower (q + 1) h` steps.
- Intermediate-size bound `S q h := tower (q + 1) (h + 1)` — every term on
  the reduction path has `Tm.size ≤ S q h` (section "Mathematical notes",
  N5).

Consumers needing a single clock read `B q h ≤ (q + 1) * tower (q + 1) h`
(corollary in Task 6.3.8); all three shapes are elementary in `h` for fixed
`q`, the spec's acceptance criterion.

### Decision P3 — the size invariant via the intersection relation

`lemma12`'s counted chain is stated over

```lean
def OneLambda.stepWithin (A) (M : ℕ) {Γ s} :
    Binding.Tm (oneLambdaSig A) Γ s → Binding.Tm (oneLambdaSig A) Γ s → Prop :=
  fun a b => OneLambdaStep a b ∧ Tm.size b ≤ M
```

(the spec §5.1 device), with a monotonicity helper
`relatesInSteps_mono : (∀ a b, r a b → r' a b) → RelatesInSteps r a b k →
RelatesInSteps r' a b k` to relax `M` upward when composing phases, and a
projection to plain `RelatesInSteps OneLambdaStep` and
`Relation.ReflTransGen OneLambdaStep`. The initial term is covered separately
by `Tm.size t ≤ 2 ^ Tm.height t` (arity ≤ 2; Task 6.3.2).

### Decision P4 — Proposition 13's normal form is identified semantically

`Represents` at sort `o` supplies an *unbounded* reduction of the bar-image
application to the value word `conc (appEval … finZeroElim)`
(`Represents.lean:139`); `lemma12` supplies a *bounded* reduction to *some*
normal form `n`. The 6.2 `lemma8` closes representation only toward
predecessors (`Fhat' ⇒* Fhat` gives `Represents F Fhat'`,
`Represents.lean:175`), so it cannot transfer representation to `n`. Joining
the two reducts by confluence of `OneLambdaStep` (β + η + ι, Tait–Martin-Löf
parallel reduction) would be a development comparable in size to the rest of
this sub-phase with no other consumer in it. Instead: define the standard
`1λ(A)` evaluator `oneEval` (the `Tm.eval`/`appEval` pattern of
`Applicative.lean:464-520`) and prove one-step soundness
(`OneLambdaStep t t' → oneEval t env = oneEval t' env`). Then `n` is a closed
normal term of sort `o`, hence a constructor word `conc b` (Task 6.3.10), and
`b = oneEval n = oneEval (start) = oneEval (conc value) = value`, so `n` *is*
the value word exactly and no η-tail or join is needed. Reason this is not
scope creep: the parent sub-plan's 6.4 lists "semantic adequacy — reduction
is sound for the standard model, and the normal form at type `o` reads off as
the denotation" among its substances; `prop13_elementary` as fixed by the
spec (§5.2) needs precisely this content, and building it here hands 6.4 its
adequacy inputs early instead of duplicating them.

### Decision P5 — a source-side constructor word `sourceWord`

Proposition 13 quantifies over `a : FreeAlg natAlgSig` and speaks of "the
denotation of `F` at `a`", which requires a closed source term of sort
`Ω τ` whose `appEval` value is `a`. No such former exists in the 6.1/6.2
layers (`conc`/`bbRep` are target-side). Task 6.3.11 adds
`sourceWord (a : FreeAlg natAlgSig) (τ : RType) :
Binding.Tm (rlmrOSig natAlgSig) [] (RType.omega τ)` with
`appEval_sourceWord : appEval (sourceWord a τ) finZeroElim = a`. Verify
absence before implementing (grep `FreeAlg` producers in
`Applicative.lean`); if an equivalent former exists, consume it instead and
record the substitution in the ledger.

### Decision P6 — `Normal` is defined as `redexRank … = 0`

The p. 224 source text couples the two notions ("the redex rank of `M` … is
0 if `M` is normal"; ranks of redexes are ≥ 1 since `ord` of an arrow type is
≥ 1). Defining `Normal t := redexRank t = 0` makes the coupling definitional
and gives the ι-phase its working criterion. The semantic anchor guarding the
detector's precision is Task 6.3.10 (closed normal sort-`o` terms are
constructor words) together with the acceptance examples. Completeness of the
detector against `OneLambdaStep` is explicitly deferred: the spec §4.3 records
the irreducibility characterization as a consequence, and the direction this
sub-phase proves is redex-to-step existence (Task 6.3.4b); the converse (no
step from a `Normal` term, η aside) is deferred to the phase that first
consumes it (candidate: 6.4's code-level normal-form detection), with the
deferral recorded here so it is visible at review.

## Mathematical notes (binding elaboration for Tasks 6.3.5-6.3.8)

The spec's §6 stages, elaborated to the invariants the Lean induction needs.
These notes are the working form of Leivant's paragraph (ii) (spec §2 pinned
form); the detailed literature is Battyányi-Nour arXiv `1703.05930` and
Schwichtenberg 1991 (spec §3). The Lean proof is the checker of record for
every claim below.

- **N1 (β-redex rank).** A β-redex `app' (lam' b) N` with `lam' b : σ ⟶ τ`
  has rank `RType.ord (σ ⟶ τ) = max (ord σ + 1) (ord τ) ≥ 1`. ι-redexes have
  rank 1 (`ord (o ⟶ o) = 1`; every function position in a saturated
  `dstr`/`case` spine has an `o^j ⟶ o` type). `betaRedexRank` tracks
  β-redexes only; `redexRank = max betaRedexRank (if an ι-redex exists then 1
  else 0)`.
- **N2 (substitution rank).** For `N : σ` and
  `b : Tm (oneLambdaSig A) (Γ ++ [σ]) τ`:
  `betaRedexRank (instantiate₁ N b) ≤
  max (betaRedexRank b) (max (betaRedexRank N) (RType.ord σ))`.
  New β-redexes arise only where the substituted variable stood in function
  position (`x M` becomes `N' M` with `N'` an abstraction); such a redex has
  rank `ord σ`. Proved by induction over `b` generalized to an
  environment-indexed statement whose invariant bounds every image's
  `betaRedexRank` and bounds the sort order only for images that are *not*
  variables — the sort of a variable image is unconstrained (a variable in
  function position forms no redex). This exemption is what makes the
  invariant stable under `Env.underBinder`: the fresh image is a variable
  (exempt, whatever the binder's sort order), and renamed old images keep
  their ranks, sorts, and non-variable status.
- **N3 (the cycle).** For `q ≥ 1`: every `t` with `betaRedexRank t ≤ q`
  admits `t ⇒^k t'` (β-steps and congruence only) with
  (1) `betaRedexRank t' ≤ q - 1`;
  (2) `Tm.height t' ≤ 2 ^ Tm.height t`;
  (3) `k ≤ Tm.size t`;
  (4) the shape invariant: if `t'` is an abstraction (`lam'`-headed) then
  `t` is an abstraction or `RType.ord s ≤ q` (`s` the sort of `t`).
  Structural induction on `t`. Variable/constant leaves and non-`app`
  operation nodes: `t' := t` (children of `con`/`dstr`/`case` nodes are
  empty; the only node with a body is `lam`, handled by congruence
  `lamBody`). Abstraction: recurse into the body. Application
  `t = app' D E` with `D : σ ⟶ τ`: recurse to `D'`, `E'`; lift by
  `reduces_app'_left`/`reduces_app'_right` (counted analogues). If `D'` is
  not an abstraction, or `ord (σ ⟶ τ) < q`: `t' := app' D' E'`; a top
  β-redex, if any, has rank `ord (σ ⟶ τ)`, which is `≤ q - 1` — when `D` is
  an abstraction because the redex was in `t` (rank ≤ q) and the contraction
  case below did not apply only when `ord < q`; when `D` is not an
  abstraction by invariant (4) on `D` (which gives `ord (σ ⟶ τ) ≤ q`)
  sharpened by the non-application condition. If `D' = lam' b` and
  `ord (σ ⟶ τ) ≥ q` (with (4) on `D` this forces `= q`): one more β-step to
  `t' := instantiate₁ E' b`; N2 bounds the new ranks by the maximum of the
  subterm ranks (each `≤ q - 1` by the inductive hypotheses) and `ord σ`,
  itself `≤ q - 1` since `ord σ + 1 ≤ ord (σ ⟶ τ) = q`;
  the height obeys
  `height (instantiate₁ E' b) ≤ height b + height E' ≤ height D' + height E'`
  and the binary-node recurrence closes (2); invariant (4) for `t'` holds
  because `ord τ ≤ ord (σ ⟶ τ) = q`.
- **N4 (downward induction).** Iterate N3 from `q₀ := betaRedexRank t` down
  to 1: β-normal `t*` with `height t* ≤ tower q₀ h` (compose (2) through
  `tower_mono_right`), total β-steps
  `≤ Σ_{i=1..q₀} tower i h ≤ q₀ * tower q₀ h` (cycle `i` starts at height
  `≤ tower (i-1) h`, so its step count is `≤ size ≤ 2 ^ tower (i-1) h =
  tower i h`).
- **N5 (intermediate sizes).** Every term on an N3 path for `t` is a hybrid
  of processed and unprocessed subterms; each is bounded by the cycle
  hypothesis's `M` with lower bound `Tm.size t + 2 ^ (2 ^ Tm.height t)`
  (the unprocessed remainder plus a processed part bounded through the
  endpoint height bound; the endpoint-sum reading fails for mid-chain
  hybrids in the contraction case, so the `hM` form is the operative one),
  hence `≤ 2 ^ h + 2 ^ (2 ^ h) ≤ 2 ^ (2 ^ h + 1)`. Across all cycles and the
  ι-phase (sizes there only decrease), every intermediate has size
  `≤ 2 * 2 ^ tower q₀ h ≤ tower (q₀ + 1) (h + 1) = S q₀ h`, using
  `tower k (x + 1) ≥ tower k x + 1` (small lemma, Task 6.3.5). The cycle
  lemma is stated over `stepWithin M` with `M` a parameter bounded below by
  the hybrid expression, and phases compose through `relatesInSteps_mono`.
- **N6 (ι-phase).** For β-normal `t` (i.e. `betaRedexRank t = 0`) that is
  not `Normal`: an ι-redex exists (definitional reading of
  `redexRank t ≠ 0` with no β contribution) and contracting it (`dstrHit`,
  `dstrMiss`, or `case`, under congruence) strictly decreases `Tm.size`,
  does not increase `Tm.height`, and preserves `betaRedexRank = 0` (the
  contractum is a subterm of sort `o` placed at a sort-`o` position, so no
  new abstraction meets an argument; the paper's parenthetical
  "`case : o^{k+1} → o`"). Strong induction on `Tm.size` yields a `Normal`
  form in `≤ Tm.size t` steps. The strict decrease is the spec §6's recorded
  strengthening of the paper's "decrease".
- **N7 (assembly).** `lemma12` composes N4 and N6 at `q := redexRank t`
  (using `betaRedexRank t ≤ redexRank t`), with the bounds of decision P2.
  `q = 0` degenerates to `n := t, k := 0`; `q = 1` runs one cycle (possibly
  vacuous) plus the ι-phase.
